Bottom line: a candidate for standardizing project delivery around owners, deadlines and dependencies

Decision summary

Asana

Asana is a work-management service for tasks, assignees, due dates, projects, dependencies, forms and status reporting. Personal is free for up to two users. Starter adds timeline and Gantt views, dashboards, automation and free guests; Advanced adds portfolios, goals, workload, approvals and time tracking.

Advantages confirmed from official sources

  • Personal supports two users with unlimited tasks and projects
  • Starter adds timeline, Gantt, dashboards, forms and unlimited automations
  • Starter and above list unlimited free guests
  • Advanced adds portfolios, goals, workload, approvals and time tracking

Constraints to confirm before purchase

  • The free plan is limited to two users, so most teams will need a paid plan
  • Starter and Advanced are per-user subscriptions with different monthly and annual prices
  • SSO, SCIM, guest-invite control and view-only licenses are Enterprise features
  • Add-ons such as Timesheets and Budgets may cost extra

Frequently asked questions

How many users are included in Asana Personal?

Personal is free for up to two users and lists unlimited tasks and projects with list, board and calendar views.

Are Starter guests billable?

The official pricing page lists unlimited free guests in Starter. Confirm the distinction between guests and organization members and their access scope.

Does Asana support SSO and SCIM?

Enterprise lists SAML authentication and SCIM provisioning. Enterprise+ adds controls such as audit logs, data residency and SIEM integrations.
