monday.com Review: Free, Basic, Standard, Pro, Pricing and Permissions

This review examines monday work management Free, Basic, Standard, Pro and Enterprise using vendor pricing and support documentation. It covers seat bundles, boards, views, automation, guests, permissions, SAML and SCIM without assigning an untested productivity score.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 14 minutesBy Luqevora.com Editorial

Method: We checked monday.com pricing, support, permission and authentication documentation on July 16, 2026. Pricing, discounts, AI credits, limits and features may change; verify the checkout screen before purchase.

Product scope: monday.com offers several products. This article primarily covers monday work management.

Bottom line: a candidate for teams that want to design workflows with boards and add automation and governance by tier

Decision summary

monday work management

The Free plan supports up to two users, three boards and three workdocs. Paid plans use seat buckets beginning at three users. Basic, Standard, Pro and Enterprise progressively add views, guests, automation, integrations, private boards, time tracking and organization-wide controls.

Confirmed advantages

  • Free plan provides a small two-user workspace for testing the basic model
  • Standard adds timeline, Gantt, calendar, guests, automations and integrations
  • Pro lists private boards, time tracking, advanced views and 25,000 monthly automation/integration actions
  • Enterprise adds multi-level permissions, portfolio and resource management, SAML and SCIM

Constraints to verify

  • Paid subscriptions start with a three-seat bucket rather than single-seat purchasing
  • Standard lists 250 monthly actions each for automations and integrations
  • Some granular permissions, including assigned-item editing, require Pro or higher
  • Enterprise pricing and contractual controls require a sales discussion

Permission and guest checks

Board owners can select permission sets such as edit everything, edit content only, view and comment, and—on Pro and above—edit assigned items only. Effective access reflects the most restrictive applicable account, workspace and board rule.

Standard includes guest access. Teams should verify which boards external people can access, when a guest becomes billable, and whether private boards are required.

When it may fit

Consider monday.com when

You need to model different workflows—projects, intake, sales, production or hiring—with configurable boards, columns and automations, or want cross-team dashboards.

Compare alternatives when

You need only a minimal Kanban board, want true per-seat purchasing for a very small team, or primarily need a document and internal-wiki platform.

Pre-purchase checklist

  • Compare actual users with the required seat bucket
  • Confirm whether needed views require Standard or Pro
  • Estimate monthly automation and integration actions
  • Separate external guests from internal members
  • Check private boards, time tracking and granular permissions
  • Confirm Enterprise if SAML or SCIM is mandatory
