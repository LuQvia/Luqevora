Verdict: a strong starting point for lowering POS software cost

AirREGI is attractive when a retailer, restaurant, or service business wants core checkout and sales management without an initial or recurring POS software charge.

The free software label is not the total cost. The business must model iPad or iPhone hardware, receipt printing, cash drawers, scanners, payment acceptance, and any AirREGI Order modules.

Pricing: the POS application is the free component

Official information states that AirREGI's initial fee, monthly fee, core functions, and standard support are free without a qualifying usage restriction.

Implementation cost depends on existing equipment and operating requirements. Decide whether the store needs printed receipts, cash storage, barcodes, customer displays, cash recycling, and additional ordering devices.

  • Provide a compatible iPad or iPhone
  • Add printers and cash hardware when required
  • AirPAY and AirREGI Order are separate services

Core functions and operational coverage

The documented feature set includes order entry, checkout, returns, product setup, cost fields, inventory, stocktaking, customer records, sales analysis, CSV exports, and multi-store management.

Connections to accounting software, AirPAY, reservation tools, and AirREGI Order allow the system to expand beyond a standalone register.

Advantages

A store can test product setup, staff training, checkout, and reporting before committing to a software subscription. Product images, barcodes, split checkout, and sales reports cover many everyday workflows.

AirPAY integration can transfer the transaction amount and reduce duplicate entry. Accounting connections can also reduce manual sales posting.

Limitations and risks

The environment is centered on compatible Apple devices, so an Android-only hardware plan will not work. Peripheral compatibility, printing, cash control, and synchronization after connectivity loss require testing.

Restaurants that need handheld ordering, kitchen workflows, or mobile ordering must price AirREGI Order separately instead of treating every ordering function as part of the free POS application.

Pre-launch checklist

Run a realistic test covering product setup, discounts, returns, split checkout, closing, inventory adjustments, staff permissions, and data export. Verify every required accounting or payment integration and its synchronization timing.

Before rollout, document who owns menu maintenance, tax settings, discounts, refunds, closing procedures, device updates, and staff training. A free application can reduce software cost, but operational quality still depends on hardware readiness, clear permissions, and a tested fallback when the tablet, printer, network, or connected payment service is unavailable.

  • First-year and renewal total cost
  • Required number of tablets, printers, and cash drawers
  • AirPAY underwriting and activation dates
  • Offline checkout and synchronization procedure
  • Export and retention after cancellation
