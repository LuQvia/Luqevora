Register functions

AirREGI documents order entry, checkout, inventory, customers, sales analysis, CSV exports, accounting connections, and offline checkout.

Square provides catalog, inventory, customers, reports, staff access, and refunds and connects those functions to online and invoice workflows.

Payment integration

AirREGI transfers amounts to AirPAY to reduce duplicate entry, but the payment contract and underwriting remain separate.

Square POS and Square payments operate under the same provider and account, simplifying hardware and payment reporting.

Advanced restaurant and retail functions

AirREGI Order, Square for Restaurants, retail plans, and other advanced modules can create additional software or hardware cost.

Do not compare only the free applications when handhelds, kitchen displays, mobile ordering, multi-store administration, or detailed inventory are required.

Run the same scenario

Configure one week of products, staff, discounts, refunds, closing, inventory, and payments in both systems and reproduce the same trading scenario.

A practical pilot should use the same products, discounts, tax rules, staff roles, refund cases, and end-of-day reports in both systems. Record setup time, checkout time, correction steps, hardware needs, and export quality. This converts a feature comparison into an operating-cost comparison and exposes training or exception-handling differences before a full migration. Rehearse the selected workflow with every role before launch.

Frequently asked questions

Should I simply choose the lowest processing rate?

No. Compare the effective cost including eligibility, payment mix, hardware, subscriptions, settlement, and integration.

Should POS and payments come from one provider?

One provider can simplify amount transfer and support, but also compare functionality, rates, and portability.

Which sales data is needed for a comparison?

Prepare at least three months of value and transaction count by card brand, e-money, QR, cash, and in-person or online channel.
