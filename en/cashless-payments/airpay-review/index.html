AirPAY accepts cards, e-money, and QR payments for Japanese merchants. It has no monthly or bank transfer fee, but the effective cost depends on payment method, tax treatment, eligibility for the 2.48% discount program, device requirements, and settlement schedule.

Verdict: best considered with AirREGI and broad local acceptance

AirPAY fits stores that want amount transfer from AirREGI and broad support for major cards, transit e-money, iD, QUICPay, and Japanese QR methods without a monthly fee.

Not every transaction uses one rate. Payment brand, tax treatment, discount underwriting, bank, and the application used can change cost and settlement timing.

How to read the fee table

The standard rate is listed as 3.24% for major cards, iD, QUICPay, and UnionPay. Transit e-money and Rakuten Edy are 2.95% before tax, COIN+ is 0.99% before tax, and many QR methods are 2.95% before tax.

The 2.48% major-card rate requires application, eligibility, and brand-level approval. Calculate a weighted effective rate from the store's actual card, e-money, and QR mix instead of relying on the lowest advertised rate.

Settlement and cash flow

AirPAY and AirPAY Tap settle six times monthly for accounts at three named major banks and three times monthly for other supported banks. Bank transfer fees are free.

Online payments and some QR workflows can follow different settlement calendars. Maintain separate closing and deposit dates for each payment function in the cash-flow forecast.

Payment coverage and hardware

The service supports card-reader transactions, transit e-money, iD, QUICPay, and multiple QR methods. Underwriting completes separately by brand, so every method may not be active on opening day.

A compatible iPad or iPhone and reader are required, with optional printing equipment. Stores using AirREGI integration should verify the supported device and account configuration before launch.

Advantages and cautions

No monthly fee, no bank transfer fee, and amount transfer from AirREGI can reduce duplicate entry for a small store. The acceptance mix is designed for payment methods commonly used in Japan.

Rates are displayed with mixed tax treatment and the discount is not automatic. Underwriting, campaigns, and reader conditions can change, so save the current application terms.

Pre-launch checklist

Segment recent sales into card, e-money, QR, and cash. Model processing cost under both standard and discounted rates, and leave enough time for brand-level underwriting before a fixed opening date.

The effective cost is not only the advertised processing percentage. Model each payment method by expected sales mix, tax treatment of fees, settlement timing, refund handling, device requirements, and accounting workload. Confirm which methods are approved for the specific merchant category and keep cash or another fallback available until all required brands are active.
