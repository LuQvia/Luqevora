Verdict: choose around the existing workflow and future channels

AirPAY is a natural fit for an AirREGI store that values Japanese e-money and QR coverage and can operate with three or six settlements monthly.

Square is easier to understand when the business wants POS and payments from one provider and expects to add online selling, invoices, or appointments.

Fee comparison: do not decide from the minimum headline

AirPAY's 2.48% and Square's 2.5% rates each have eligibility conditions. Other brands, e-money, QR, online, and manually entered transactions can use different rates, so a weighted sales model is required.

A zero monthly fee does not eliminate iPad, reader, terminal, printer, connectivity, or industry-software cost. Compare a three-year total.

Settlement and working capital

AirPAY deposits six times monthly for selected major banks and three times monthly for other supported banks. Square documents next-business-day deposits for two named banks and weekly Friday deposits for other supported banks.

A business with short supplier or payroll cycles may value settlement speed more than a small processing-rate difference. Model the maximum outstanding balance.

Hardware and counter design

AirPAY combines a compatible Apple device and reader and can transfer the amount from AirREGI, helping unify checkout for an AirREGI user.

Square offers a small reader, a stand, and a printer-equipped terminal, with published prices that simplify the initial hardware budget.

Best-fit stores

AirPAY fits AirREGI users, stores emphasizing domestic e-money and QR methods, and Apple-device operations.

Square fits businesses that want to integrate POS, payments, ecommerce, and invoices over time or select dedicated hardware with published pricing.

Build a comparable model

Segment the last three months of sales by payment method, apply the relevant rate, and add devices, tablets, printers, subscriptions, paid features, and the cash-flow effect of settlement timing.

Frequently asked questions

Should I simply choose the lowest processing rate?

No. Compare the effective cost including eligibility, payment mix, hardware, subscriptions, settlement, and integration.

Should POS and payments come from one provider?

One provider can simplify amount transfer and support, but also compare functionality, rates, and portability.

Which sales data is needed for a comparison?

Prepare at least three months of value and transaction count by card brand, e-money, QR, cash, and in-person or online channel.
