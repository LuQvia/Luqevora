How to compare pricing

Square publishes its standard POS price, device prices, and major processing rates. PayCAS Mobile quotes setup, monthly, terminal, processing, and transaction charges for each merchant.

Convert both options into a three-year total covering device count, connectivity, paper, applications, replacement, cancellation, and settlement.

Mobility and connectivity

Square Terminal is portable, while a small reader requires a compatible phone or tablet. Verify the network and charging configuration for the selected hardware.

PayCAS Mobile integrates a SoftBank SIM and printer and is designed to operate without local wired LAN or Wi-Fi. Test coverage and establish a fallback device process.

Settlement and working capital

Square settles next business day for selected banks or weekly for other supported banks. PayCAS uses a twice-monthly cycle.

Fewer deposits create a larger outstanding balance. Businesses with frequent supplier payments should model working-capital cost alongside processing fees.

POS and business functions

Square expands into POS, inventory, customers, online selling, invoices, and appointments.

PayCAS Mobile is payment-terminal centered and adds functions through PayCAS DX Store applications or external POS integration. Confirm formal support for the required POS.

Hardware test plan

Test payment, cancellation, reprinting, daily totals, charging, and portability in the same location and network environment.

Compare both options with an identical transaction scenario and a full-year cost model. Include device purchase or rental, monthly connectivity, processing by payment mix, receipt supplies, settlement timing, replacement support, and cancellation conditions. A lower visible fee may not be the better choice when the required counter hardware or reconciliation workflow adds recurring labor.

Frequently asked questions

Should I simply choose the lowest processing rate?

No. Compare the effective cost including eligibility, payment mix, hardware, subscriptions, settlement, and integration.

Should POS and payments come from one provider?

One provider can simplify amount transfer and support, but also compare functionality, rates, and portability.

Which sales data is needed for a comparison?

Prepare at least three months of value and transaction count by card brand, e-money, QR, cash, and in-person or online channel.
