1Password Teams Starter Pack vs Business: Pricing, Seats, Admin and Identity Integrations

Teams Starter Pack uses a package price that includes ten members, while Business uses per-user pricing and adds identity-provider integrations and broader administration. The correct choice depends on headcount and access-governance requirements.

Published and checked: July 23, 2026Estimated reading time: 10 minBy Luqevora.com Editorial Team

Checked: July 23, 2026, using 1Password's official pricing and product pages. Prices are displayed in USD with annual billing and can vary by region, taxes and promotions.

Affiliate status: No 1Password affiliate link is active on this page at publication. Links go to official provider pages.

Verdict: Teams fits a basic ten-person workflow; Business is the comparison point for identity integrations and governance

Decision summary

Do not choose by headcount alone

Teams Starter Pack is displayed at US$24.95 per month including ten members with annual billing. Business is US$8.99 per user per month with annual billing and adds integrations with Okta, Entra ID, OneLogin, Duo and broader business administration.

Cost considerations as the team grows

Teams Starter Pack includes ten members. The official pricing page also states that additional seats can be added at per-member pricing. Verify the final additional-seat price in checkout.

Business scales by user. Model current employees, expected 12-month growth, contractors, guests and administrators. Do not calculate a break-even point until the additional Teams seat price and contract terms are confirmed.

Which plan fits which workflow?

Teams Starter Pack

  • About ten team members
  • Shared vaults and role permissions are the main need
  • No complex identity integration requirement
  • Package pricing is preferred

Business

  • Headcount is growing
  • IdP, SSO or provisioning is required
  • Joiner, mover and leaver processes need standardization
  • Administration and audit requirements are stronger

Deployment checklist

  • Current headcount and 12-month growth plan.
  • Vault ownership and administrator roles.
  • Joiner, mover and leaver access procedures.
  • Need for Okta, Entra ID or another identity provider.
  • Emergency recovery, Secret Key and admin-account custody.

Frequently asked questions

Do teams above ten users have to move to Business?

The official page says additional Teams seats can be added. Compare the checkout price, required administration and expected growth with Business.

What is the main Business difference?

Identity-provider integrations and broader organizational administration are the main reasons to compare Business.

Is a free trial available?

The official pricing page lists a 14-day free trial for both plans. Verify current signup terms.
