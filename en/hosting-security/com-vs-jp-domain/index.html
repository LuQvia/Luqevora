Three-point summary

  1. The choice is mainly about audience, brand, eligibility, and long-term administration—not a guaranteed ranking advantage.
  2. Choose .com for a broadly international or market-neutral brand.
  3. .com is a generic top-level domain used globally, while .jp is Japan's country-code domain with policies and categories administered for the Japanese namespace.
Key decision

Decision summary

Choose .com for a broadly international or market-neutral brand. Choose .jp when a clear Japan connection is valuable and the registrant meets the relevant eligibility rules. Some organizations defensively register both.

Strengths and trade-offs

Registering both can protect a brand but adds renewal and DNS governance. Using one as primary requires a redirect and email plan for the other.

  • Strength: A domain can reinforce audience and brand positioning
  • Strength: Both support standard web and email use when configured correctly
  • Verify: Category-specific .jp eligibility
  • Verify: Renewal ownership, DNS security, and expiration recovery

Who it suits

Select from the primary audience and legal or operational presence of the brand.

  • .com: international brands and products
  • .jp: Japan-focused organizations and services
  • Both: brands protecting key names across markets
