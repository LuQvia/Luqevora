Hostinger Review: Premium, Unlimited and Cloud Startup Pricing, Renewals and Backups

This Hostinger review checks the advertised term, renewal rate, sites, storage, backup frequency and migration conditions—not only the promotional monthly equivalent.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time:11 minBy Luqevora.com Editorial

Research method: We checked the provider’s official pricing, help, security, backup and migration materials on July 16, 2026. Pricing, promotions, renewal rates, limits and regional availability can change; verify the checkout terms before purchasing.

Scope: This review organizes official information for purchase decisions. We did not run a reproducible speed benchmark or long-term support test, so we do not independently rank performance, uptime or support quality.

Affiliate disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be identified near the link and under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Conclusion: low introductory equivalents with long prepayment, requiring close renewal and backup checks

Decision summary

Hostinger

Hostinger combines managed web hosting, WordPress and a website builder in hPanel. At the time checked, Premium is advertised at a $2.99 monthly equivalent for 48 months and renews at $10.99/month. Compare the total upfront payment, renewal rate and the duration of included domain and email offers.

Advantages confirmed from official information

  • Free SSL and automatic migration are listed from Premium
  • Unlimited lists unlimited sites, 50GB NVMe and daily backups
  • Cloud Startup lists 100GB NVMe plus daily and on-demand backups
  • 24/7 support and a 99.9% uptime guarantee are stated

Constraints to verify before buying

  • The lowest displayed rate requires 48 months prepaid
  • Renewal rates are materially higher than the introductory equivalents
  • Free domain and mailbox benefits have time conditions
  • Official material alone cannot establish independent speed or support rankings

Backup frequency depends on the plan

Premium

The pricing page lists weekly backups. Sites with frequent orders or reservations should verify whether weekly restore points meet their recovery needs.

Unlimited and Cloud Startup

Unlimited lists daily backups, while Cloud Startup lists daily and on-demand backups. For critical data, also verify an independent export or external copy.

Operational note: Do not rely on a single provider-side backup for critical business data.

Who may be a fit—and who should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • A small site can use a long term to reduce introductory cost
  • You want domain, SSL, email and migration in one service
  • You want WordPress or a builder managed in one dashboard
  • You need to compare higher plans for multiple sites

Compare alternatives when

  • You want month-to-month flexibility without long prepayment
  • You want little difference between introductory and renewal pricing
  • You require root access or dedicated resources
  • You prioritize specialist managed WordPress operations

Pre-purchase checklist

  1. 1. Confirm the term and total upfront payment
  2. 2. Calculate multi-year cost using the renewal rate
  3. 3. Set required site, storage and backup frequency
  4. 4. Check domain and mailbox cost after free periods
  5. 5. Verify migration eligibility, data center and refund terms

Frequently asked questions

Is the advertised $2.99 Premium price month-to-month?

No. The checked offer is a monthly equivalent for 48 months paid in advance. The official page states a $10.99/month renewal rate.

What is the main difference between Premium and Unlimited?

At the time checked, Premium includes three sites, 20GB SSD and weekly backups; Unlimited includes unlimited sites, 50GB NVMe and daily backups.

Does Hostinger offer free migration?

Hostinger states that website migration is free after purchasing a hosting plan. Confirm eligibility and the migration method for your site before relying on it.
