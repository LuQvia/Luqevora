Keeper Review: Business Starter, Business and Enterprise Sharing, Admin and SSO

We review Keeper business plans by separating Business Starter for small teams, Business for expanded organizational administration, and Enterprise for advanced provisioning and identity integration.

Published and reviewed: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora.com Editorial

Research method: We reviewed the provider's official pricing, product, security, and administration documentation on July 16, 2026. Pricing, features, minimum seats, and regional availability can change, so verify the final checkout or sales terms before purchasing.

Review scope: This article organizes official information for procurement decisions. We did not conduct independent penetration tests, long-term operational testing, or support-response benchmarking, so we do not independently rank security performance or usability.

Advertising disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be disclosed near the link and handled under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Conclusion: Starter for 5–10 users, Business for organizational controls, Enterprise for advanced IdP integration

Decision summary

Keeper

Business Starter is positioned for 5–10 users and includes an encrypted vault, admin console, credential sharing, and autofill. Business adds shared team folders and delegated administration. Enterprise adds SCIM, AD/LDAP, SAML SSO, RBAC, and developer APIs. Because public pricing varies by region and purchase flow, verify checkout or a formal quote.

Advantages confirmed in official information

  • A clearly defined Business Starter tier for 5–10 users
  • Shared team folders and delegated administration in Business
  • SCIM, AD/LDAP, SAML SSO, RBAC, and APIs in Enterprise
  • Zero-knowledge encryption and multiple security certifications are documented

Constraints to verify before purchase

  • Public prices are dynamic; checkout or a quote is required
  • Advanced reporting, BreachWatch, PAM, and other capabilities may be add-ons
  • Enterprise capabilities may be excessive for very small teams
  • IdP, SIEM, and PAM integrations require implementation planning and ownership

Organizations that may fit—and those that should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • Small teams of 5–10 that want a staged upgrade path
  • Organizations prioritizing shared folders and delegated administration
  • Companies requiring AD/LDAP, SCIM, or SAML SSO
  • Organizations that may later evaluate secrets management or PAM on the same platform

Should compare alternatives

  • Buyers that require a fully public price for immediate comparison
  • Teams below the minimum seat range that need business controls
  • Organizations without staff to manage IdP and directory integrations
  • Buyers expecting advanced PAM to be included without add-ons

Frequently asked questions

How many users is Keeper Business Starter designed for?

The official pricing page states that Business Starter protects teams of 5–10 users. Larger organizations should compare Business and Enterprise.

How much does Keeper cost?

The official page uses dynamic regional and purchase-flow pricing, and a single reliable amount was not available in the public page text reviewed. Verify checkout or a sales quote.

Does Keeper offer a free trial?

Keeper advertises free trials for Business, Enterprise, and KeeperPAM. Its Enterprise trial page states that no credit card is required.
