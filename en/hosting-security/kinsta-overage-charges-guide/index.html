Kinsta keeps sites running after many plan limits are exceeded, but charges differ for visits, server bandwidth, storage, and CDN bandwidth.

Bottom line: budget the overage rate, not only the monthly plan

Cost control

Use 80% and 100% alerts

Kinsta plans include allowances for visits or server bandwidth, SSD storage, and CDN bandwidth. The documentation describes alerts at 80% and 100% of plan usage.

Visits
$0.50 per 1,000
Bandwidth
$0.50 per GB
Storage

CDN

Documented overage rates

ResourceRateMonitor

Alerts and upgrade timing

Plan-limit notifications are enabled by default and are sent at 80% and 100% usage. Visit-based plans also document a notification when usage exceeds the allowance by 10%. An upgrade is not applied retroactively, so overages incurred before the change remain billable.

Operating rule: review Plan usage in MyKinsta regularly rather than waiting for email alerts.

How to estimate overage cost

  1. 1.Review three to six months of visits, bandwidth, and storage.
  2. 2.Add expected growth from campaigns and seasonal peaks.
  3. 3.Multiply the expected excess by the documented rate.
  4. 4.Compare the result with the next plan’s price difference.
  5. 5.Evaluate caching, CDN, image optimization, and file cleanup.

FAQ

Does Kinsta stop a site at the limit?

The normal policy is to keep sites running and charge overages, although extreme overages or payment issues can lead to restrictions.

Does upgrading remove earlier overages?

No. The upgrade is not retroactive.

Where can usage be monitored?

Use the Plan usage view in the MyKinsta dashboard.
