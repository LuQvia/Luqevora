5 Business Password Managers for Small Teams: Pricing, Sharing and Admin Controls Compared

Business credential sharing requires more than a low subscription price. Permission separation, offboarding, audit logs, SSO, SCIM and recovery procedures all affect the decision. This article compares five products using official information under the same criteria.

Use-case summary

Fixed package for up to 10 people

1Password states that its Teams Starter Pack protects up to 10 members for $24.95 per month, making the total easier to estimate for a stable small team.

Per-user entry point

Bitwarden Teams is listed at $4 per user per month billed annually, while Enterprise is $6. Teams already includes sharing, event logs, directory sync and SCIM.

Organizational structure and advanced controls

Keeper separates Business Starter, Business and Enterprise, with higher tiers adding delegated administration, SCIM, AD/LDAP, SAML SSO, RBAC and APIs.

Free entry and staged expansion

Zoho Vault offers Free, Standard, Professional and Enterprise plans. Its 15-day Enterprise trial falls back to the free plan with data retained when no paid upgrade is selected.

Official product details and decision checks

1Password

1Password states that its Teams Starter Pack protects up to 10 team members for $24.95 per month. It includes unlimited items in encrypted vaults, 24/7 support and end-to-end protection.

  • Fixed pack for up to 10 members
  • Password and passkey creation and storage
  • Shared vaults for onboarding and offboarding
  • Unlimited items and 24/7 support

Before buying: confirm the plan required after 10 users, administrative features, SSO/provisioning and billing term.

Bitwarden

Bitwarden lists Teams at $4 per user per month billed annually and Enterprise at $6. Teams includes secure sharing, event logs, directory sync and SCIM.

  • Per-user Teams plan with organization management
  • Enterprise adds granular access and passwordless SSO
  • Enterprise adds account recovery and self-hosting
  • Enterprise includes a Families plan for every user

Before buying: confirm annual billing, SSO architecture and the maintenance, backup and update responsibilities of self-hosting.

Read the full Bitwarden review →

Keeper

Keeper Business Starter targets sole proprietors and teams of 5–10 users, with encrypted vaults, an admin console, sharing and autofill. Prices on the public page vary dynamically by region.

  • Business adds shared team folders and delegated administration
  • Enterprise adds SCIM, AD/LDAP and SAML SSO
  • Enterprise adds RBAC, advanced MFA and APIs
  • Free trials are offered for Business and Enterprise

Before buying: verify minimum users, annual price, add-ons such as BreachWatch and the Enterprise quote.

Read the full Keeper review →

NordPass

NordPass Teams is for small teams of up to 10 users and is sold as a 10-user pack. Business and Enterprise have a five-user minimum, and a 14-day Business trial requires no payment card.

  • Teams includes sharing, offline access, activity monitoring and MFA
  • Teams supports Google Workspace SSO
  • Business adds group/folder sharing, health and breach monitoring
  • Enterprise adds Entra ID, ADFS, Okta, Sentinel and Splunk integrations

Before buying: confirm monthly, annual or two-year terms, the 10-user package, the Business-to-Teams downgrade restriction and 14-day refund terms.

Read the full NordPass review →

Zoho Vault

Zoho Vault offers Free, Standard, Professional and Enterprise plans. Its 15-day Enterprise trial supports up to five users and falls back to the free plan with data retained when no paid upgrade is selected.

  • Standard adds team sharing, roles, administration and IP restrictions
  • Professional adds groups, shared folders, break-glass access and breach alerts
  • Enterprise adds AD, cloud-app SSO, approval workflows and SIEM
  • Annual subscriptions receive a 10% discount; Professional and Enterprise require five users

Before buying: confirm regional pricing, Free-versus-Standard sharing, directory/SSO integrations and export procedures.

Start with per-user pricing

Use Bitwarden Teams as a baseline and move to Enterprise only when SSO, recovery, self-hosting or more granular controls are required.

Frequently asked questions

Does a business password manager let every employee see every password?

No. Products can separate access through shared vaults, folders, collections, groups and roles. Confirm the exact permission model for the selected plan.

Do small teams need SSO or SCIM?

Not always, but they become important when staff changes are frequent, an identity provider is already in use, or access removal should be automated. Check plan and integration requirements.

Can a company use only a free plan for shared passwords?

It depends on the product. A free personal vault may exist while organization management, auditing, sharing and provisioning require a paid business plan.
