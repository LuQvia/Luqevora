RoboForm vs Bitwarden: Free Plan, Price, Open Source and Family Use

Bitwarden is the stronger open-source and low-cost free-access choice. RoboForm is the stronger form-filling and identity-profile choice for users who prefer its workflow.

Published and reviewed: July 24, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora.com Editorial Team

Research method: We reviewed the providers’ official product, pricing, security and help documentation on July 24, 2026. Prices, features, regional availability and promotions can change, so verify the final checkout and terms before purchase.

Bottom line

Choose Bitwarden for open-source clients, a broad free personal tier and the lower published Premium price. Choose RoboForm when detailed form filling, identity profiles and its specific autofill workflow are more important.

Bitwarden is also the clearer route for self-hosting and organizations that want open-source transparency. RoboForm remains competitive for individuals who prioritize convenience over infrastructure control.

Free-plan and paid-price differences

Bitwarden’s free personal plan is difficult to beat for users who need password access across multiple devices. RoboForm’s free plan is better viewed as a one-device evaluation or long-term single-device option.

Bitwarden Premium is also cheaper at the published annual price. RoboForm must therefore justify the difference through better fit for form filling, identities, support preferences or a specific workflow.

Features and workflow

Both products generate, save and fill passwords and support passkeys and secure sharing. RoboForm places more emphasis on form filling and saved identities. Bitwarden emphasizes open-source transparency, collections, organizations and optional self-hosting.

Do not assume one autofill engine is universally better. Test the same banking, shopping, government and multi-step login pages in both products.

Security and transparency

RoboForm states AES-256 encryption, PBKDF2 SHA-256 and zero knowledge. Bitwarden’s open-source model allows public code inspection and supports self-hosting, but self-hosting transfers operational responsibility to the user or organization.

For most individuals, correct setup, unique Master Passwords, 2FA and protected recovery methods will matter more than self-hosting.

Moving from Bitwarden to RoboForm

RoboForm’s official guide exports the Bitwarden vault to CSV, imports it through the RoboForm browser extension and then requires the plaintext export to be deleted.

Check item counts, custom fields, folders, notes and attachments before cancelling the old service.

Which should you choose?

Choose RoboForm

  • Form filling is a top requirement
  • You prefer its identity workflow
  • One-device free use is enough
  • You value its support options

Choose Bitwarden

  • You need broad free multi-device use
  • Open source matters
  • You want the lower published Premium price
  • Self-hosting is a real requirement

Frequently asked questions

Which has the stronger free plan?

Bitwarden Free is generally broader for multi-device password access. RoboForm Free lists unlimited passwords on one device.

Which paid plan is cheaper?

Bitwarden Premium was listed at $19.80 per year and RoboForm Premium renewal at $29.88 per year on July 24, 2026. Features and promotions differ.

Can I move from Bitwarden to RoboForm?

Yes. RoboForm publishes a Bitwarden CSV export and import guide and warns users to delete the unencrypted CSV after migration.
