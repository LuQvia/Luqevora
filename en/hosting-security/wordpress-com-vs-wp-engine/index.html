How to compare price and plan limits

WordPress.com starts free. Paid tiers add custom domains, ad removal, themes, plugins, backups, and commerce, with meaningful differences between monthly and multi-year billing.

Verify first-term and renewal pricing, sites, visits, bandwidth, storage, phone support, plugin updates, WAF, performance features, additional sites, and overages.

Compare WordPress.com and WP Engine by the annual total of the lowest plan that meets every required limit, not the headline price alone.

  • Introductory and renewal totals for the full commitment
  • Limits for sites, storage, traffic, devices, or users
  • Coverage for backups, restoration, and migration
  • Security features, overage charges, and support terms

Features and day-to-day workflow

Users manage WordPress posts, pages, themes, and plugins while the platform handles underlying hosting and maintenance.

After migration, teams operate production, staging, and development environments, backups, updates, caching, access, and support from a managed portal.

Frequently asked questions

Which is easier for beginners, WordPress.com or WP Engine?

Best fit for WordPress.com: Users who want WordPress extensibility with less server administration. Best fit for WP Engine: Businesses and agencies that want specialist WordPress performance, updates, and incident support. Compare both under the same free or trial conditions.

Can I compare them using free plans?

A WordPress.com or WP Engine free plan or trial can reveal the core workflow, but also check paid-plan limits, administration, export, and support on their official comparison pages.

What should I verify before migrating?

For WordPress.com and WP Engine, verify export formats, history and attachment coverage, permission mapping, integration reauthorization, and the cancellation date for the old service.
