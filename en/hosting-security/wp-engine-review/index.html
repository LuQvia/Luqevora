WP Engine Review: Pricing, Visits, Backups and Staging Explained

WP Engine Essential managed WordPress plans reviewed through official pricing, site, visit, storage, bandwidth, backup and support documentation.

Published and checked: 2026-07-16Estimated reading time: 11 minBy Luqevora.com Editorial Team

Research method: We checked WP Engine's official pricing and backup-and-restore documentation on July 16, 2026. First-year offers, coupons, renewal pricing, tax, overages and extensions can change, so the checkout screen takes precedence.

Review scope: This page summarizes published plans, limits, backups, security and development environments. We did not independently benchmark speed, uptime or support quality.

Affiliate disclosure: This page contains no affiliate links at publication. Any future compensated link will be disclosed next to the link and under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Verdict: managed WordPress hosting for teams that want backups, staging and specialist support in one platform

Decision summary

WP Engine

WP Engine is managed WordPress hosting. The official plan page lists Startup from $30 per month with one site, an estimated 25,000 monthly visits, 10GB storage and 75GB bandwidth. Daily and on-demand backups, staging, SSL, SSH and WordPress support are included, but first-year terms, renewal price, visits, bandwidth, extra sites and paid extensions require confirmation.

Backup, restore and portability

Storage and protection

WP Engine provides automatic and manual backups for production, staging and development environments. Its support documentation states that backups are stored offsite on Amazon S3 and encrypted in transit and at rest.

Restore and download

Thirty days of checkpoints are retained. Full or partial backups can be prepared as ZIP files, and the generated download link remains available for seven days. A backup point can also be restored to another environment.

External-copy note: WP Engine states that its generated backups cannot be automatically exported to another service. Businesses requiring an independent copy should plan scheduled downloads or a separate backup integration.

Who may benefit and who should compare alternatives

Potential fit

  • Teams moving WordPress updates, backups and caching to a specialist platform
  • Businesses separating production, staging and development
  • Agencies managing multiple WordPress sites in one portal
  • Organizations that value WordPress-specific support

Compare alternatives

  • You need non-WordPress applications in the same hosting account
  • You want mailboxes and domains bundled with hosting
  • You prefer not to manage visit or bandwidth limits
  • You are seeking the lowest-cost shared hosting

Frequently asked questions

How much is WP Engine Startup?

The official plan page lists Startup from $30 per month. Confirm new-customer first-year terms, tax, overages and renewal pricing at checkout.

How long are backups retained?

WP Engine's official support documentation states that daily and manual backups are available for all environments and that 30 days of checkpoints are retained.

Can backups be downloaded?

Full or partial backups covering files and database content can be prepared as ZIP files. The prepared download link remains available for seven days.
