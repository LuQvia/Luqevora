WooCommerce

An open-source ecommerce platform for WordPress with merchant control over hosting, payments, extensions, and data

2official sources
8related articles

Overview

Teams install WooCommerce on WordPress, configure products, payments, shipping, tax, and extensions, and own update testing, backups, and incident response.

Pricing

Instead of one subscription, annualize hosting, backups, security, payments, paid extensions, production, and maintenance time. Include renewals and compatibility testing.

Best fit

Stores that value WordPress flexibility and data control and can own technical operations internally or through a partner

Documented strengths

  • Open-source control over the store and data
  • Choice of hosting, payments, and features
  • Integration with WordPress content

Cautions before purchase

  • More responsibility for maintenance and security
  • Extension cost and compatibility risk
  • Total cost varies widely by architecture
