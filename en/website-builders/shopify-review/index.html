Shopify Review: Trial, Plans, Payments, Domains and Data Exports

This review organizes Shopify’s trial, plans, product and inventory management, orders, payments, domains, hosting, TLS, CSV exports and additional fees using official documentation only. It does not independently score sales performance or ease of use.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora.com Editorial Team

Method: We reviewed official pricing, help, security and migration documentation on July 16, 2026. Prices, plans, availability and features can change, so reconfirm them at checkout.

Scope: This is an official-source review for purchase decisions. We did not perform a reproducible usability or performance test, so it does not rank ease of use, speed or design quality.

Conclusion: a strong candidate when commerce is the core business requirement

Decision summary

Shopify

Shopify is a commerce platform for managing an online storefront, products, inventory, orders, payments, shipping, analytics and sales channels from one admin. It starts with a trial rather than a permanent free plan, and selling through the full online store requires an eligible paid plan. Payment availability, card rates, third-party transaction fees and app costs should be included in the decision.

Advantages confirmed in official documentation

  • Manage products, inventory, orders, payments and shipping from one admin
  • Custom domains, secure hosting and free TLS/SSL are included
  • Eligible plans include unlimited products and bandwidth
  • Products and orders can be exported as CSV files

Constraints to check before subscribing

  • There is no permanent free plan; a paid subscription is required after the trial
  • Shopify Payments and payment methods vary by country or region
  • Third-party transaction fees may apply when an external payment provider is used
  • Themes, apps, POS, shipping and international operations can add cost

Product and order data can be exported, but a full store migration must be planned component by component

Products and orders

Official documentation provides CSV exports for products and orders. Product CSV files can exchange catalog information with other platforms, and order CSV files include order, payment and fulfillment fields.

Themes, apps and settings

CSV exports are not a complete clone of themes, app settings, integrations, payment contracts, shipping rules and tax configuration. Each area needs a separate migration plan.

Operational note: For future portability, inventory products, customers, orders, images, blog content, themes, domains, payments and app integrations separately.

Who may fit and who should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • Online selling is the main business or an important channel
  • Products, inventory, orders and shipping should be managed together
  • Online, social and in-person POS channels need coordination
  • Apps and themes will be added as the business grows

Compare alternatives

  • The requirement is only a small brochure and contact site
  • Recurring platform and app costs must be minimized
  • Required payment methods are unavailable in the business region
  • You need complete server-level control of the system architecture

Pre-purchase checklist

  1. 1. Confirm Shopify Payments and required payment methods in the business region
  2. 2. Calculate card processing, third-party transaction, app, theme and POS costs
  3. 3. Document product, inventory, shipping, tax and refund workflows
  4. 4. Define staff permissions and partner access
  5. 5. Verify export and migration procedures for products, orders, customers and domains

Frequently asked questions

Does Shopify have a permanent free plan?

No. The official pricing page showed a three-day trial when checked. Reconfirm any introductory promotion and standard pricing at checkout.

Can Shopify use a custom domain and SSL?

Yes. You can connect an existing domain or buy one, and Shopify provides a free TLS/SSL certificate for domains added to the store.

Do third-party payment providers create extra fees?

Third-party transaction fees may apply based on the Shopify plan when an external payment provider is used. These fees are separate from the payment provider’s own processing fees.
