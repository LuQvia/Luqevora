At a glance

Three-point summary

  1. Shopify starts from store operations; Wix starts from a flexible business website that can include commerce.
  2. Choose Shopify when products, checkout, orders, inventory, channels, and commerce growth are central.
  3. Both build hosted websites and stores, but Shopify's admin is organized around commerce operations while Wix offers a broader visual site and business-feature model.
Key decision

Decision summary

Choose Shopify when products, checkout, orders, inventory, channels, and commerce growth are central. Choose Wix when the site must combine content, services, bookings, forms, and a smaller store with free-form visual editing.

  • Wix: visual pages, content, bookings, forms, memberships, marketing, and commerce
  • Both: themes, domains, payments, analytics, apps, and plan-specific collaboration

Pricing and plan limits

Model the intended plan with revenue, payment method, transaction terms, required apps, theme, staff, POS, bookings, email, and migration.

For Shopify and Wix, identify the lowest plan containing every required feature and compare the total for the full billing period.

  • Record introductory and renewal pricing separately
  • Compare monthly, annual, and longer-term totals
  • Check limits for seats, storage, sites, sends, or usage
  • Review refund, cancellation, and data-retention terms

Strengths and trade-offs

Shopify may be excessive for light selling; Wix may need more operational work as catalog and channel complexity grow.

  • Strength: Clear distinction between commerce-first and site-first
  • Strength: Both support a realistic trial store
  • Verify: Regional payment and plan differences
  • Verify: App dependency and platform migration

Who it suits

Decide whether selling operations or the wider site is the primary product.

  • Shopify: stores with growth and operational depth
  • Wix: service businesses and mixed-purpose sites
  • Teams able to test checkout and refund workflows

Implementation checklist

Pilot Shopify and Wix in a small scope and document cost, workflow, migration, and administration before rollout.

  • Define the objective and completion criteria in one sentence
  • Reproduce a representative task in a free plan or trial
  • Test administrator, contributor, and viewer permissions
  • Perform a real backup or export test
  • Record the renewal date and internal owner
