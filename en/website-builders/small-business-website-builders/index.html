5 Website Builders for Small Businesses Compared: Free Options, Domains and Ecommerce

Wix, Squarespace, WordPress.com, Webflow and Shopify are compared using vendor information on free access, custom domains, publishing, ecommerce and SSL. This is not a hands-on performance ranking.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 14 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial

Research method: We checked official pricing pages, plan guides, help documentation and security pages on July 16, 2026. Prices vary by country, currency, tax, billing cycle and promotions, so the comparison focuses on the underlying plan structure.

Audience: Small companies, local businesses, independent professionals and service providers building or operating a business website or online store.

Affiliate disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be identified near the link and handled under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Conclusion: narrow the list by business model and site owner

General business sites

Wix

A candidate for starting on a free site and adding a custom domain, bookings, forms and selling within one platform.

Design and selling

Squarespace

A candidate for testing a design-led site and selling products, services, memberships or content on a paid plan.

Publishing and extensions

WordPress.com

A candidate for content-heavy sites that may later use paid-plan plugins or the Commerce plan.

Design control and CMS

Webflow

A candidate for structured CMS projects built on the free Starter tier and published through a paid Site plan.

Online stores

Shopify

A commerce-first candidate for managing products, inventory, orders, payments, shipping and sales channels.

“Free access” does not mean every production requirement is free. Confirm custom domains, branding, ecommerce, collaboration, CMS limits, backups, apps and payment fees for the selected plan.

Five checks before choosing

01

Primary objective

Decide whether the priority is company information, lead generation, publishing, memberships or online selling.

02

Site owner

Identify who will update pages, posts, products, images and forms, and review permissions and editing workflows.

03

Total cost

Include domains, email, apps, themes, payment fees, implementation and maintenance—not only the subscription.

04

Migration and data

Check how pages, posts, products, orders, customers, images and form submissions can be exported.

05

Operational requirements

Review SEO, accessibility, performance, backups, SSL, support and third-party integrations.

Official facts and decision points

Wix

Wix lets users start with a free site. Its official plan guide says Premium plans include custom-domain connection, 24/7 customer support and removal of Wix branding. Selling and payment processing require an eligible business plan.

  • Build and test on a free Wix URL
  • Paid plan required for a custom domain
  • HTTPS/SSL is included for Wix sites
  • Confirm the plan for bookings, forms, payments and multilingual sites

Check before subscribing: storage, collaborators, form limits, payments, bookings, multilingual support and paid apps.

Read the detailed Wix review

Squarespace

Squarespace starts with a trial rather than a permanent free plan. Official pricing information says every paid plan can sell products or services, with different commerce features by plan. Eligible annual subscriptions include one custom domain free for one year.

  • Use the trial to evaluate editing and templates
  • All paid plans can sell, with plan-specific features
  • Eligible annual plans include a first-year domain
  • Correctly connected domains receive automatic free SSL

Check before subscribing: transaction fees, memberships, scheduling, email marketing, form integrations and custom-code needs.

Read the detailed Squarespace review

WordPress.com

WordPress.com is a hosted WordPress service with a free plan. Annual paid plans include a domain free for the first year. Official documentation reviewed in June 2026 states that Personal, Premium, Business and Commerce plans can install plugins.

  • Start pages and posts on the free plan
  • Paid plans add ad removal, a custom domain and plugins
  • The Commerce plan is designed for WooCommerce selling
  • Every WordPress.com site includes free HTTPS/SSL

Check before subscribing: Do not confuse WordPress.com with self-hosted WordPress.org. Confirm theme and plugin compatibility and any third-party costs.

Read the detailed WordPress.com review

Webflow

Webflow provides a free Starter Site plan. Its official pricing documentation separates Workspaces, where sites are staged and collaborators work, from Site plans, which provide publishing, hosting and site-level features.

  • Build and test on the free Starter tier
  • Paid Site plan required for custom-domain publishing
  • SSL hosting is provided
  • Paid Site plans support unlimited backups and restoration

Check before subscribing: Distinguish Site and Workspace plans, then review CMS items, bandwidth, forms, collaboration, code export and Ecommerce requirements.

Read the detailed Webflow review

Shopify

Shopify is an all-in-one commerce platform for storefronts, payments, products, inventory, orders, shipping and sales channels. It does not offer a permanent free plan; the official Japanese pricing page currently advertises a three-day trial and a limited introductory offer.

  • Commerce-focused store and operations
  • Connect an existing domain or buy one
  • Secure hosting, unlimited bandwidth and free TLS/SSL
  • Third-party payment provider fees may apply

Check before subscribing: Shopify Payments availability, processing fees, apps, themes, POS, shipping, tax and international selling requirements.

Read the detailed Shopify review

How to narrow the choice

Launch a company or local-business site quickly

Start with Wix and Squarespace, then compare forms, bookings, blogging and the editing workflow for the person responsible for updates.

Publish a growing content library

Compare WordPress.com posts, themes and plugins with Webflow CMS and the blogging features in Wix and Squarespace. Check categories, authors, search, redirects and structured-data controls.

Make online selling the core business

Use Shopify as a commerce benchmark, then compare Wix, Squarespace, WordPress.com Commerce and Webflow Ecommerce on payments, inventory, shipping, tax, refunds and apps.

Use a designer or agency for a custom system

Compare Webflow’s design and CMS model with WordPress.com themes and plugins and Wix Studio. Decide who will maintain the site after launch.

Frequently asked questions

Can a free plan use a custom domain?

Not all free plans or trials support publishing on a custom domain. Wix, WordPress.com and Webflow require a paid plan for custom-domain publishing. Squarespace and Shopify provide trials rather than permanent free plans.

Is Shopify always the right choice for an online store?

Shopify is commerce-first, but Wix, Squarespace, WordPress.com and Webflow also offer selling features. Compare payment fees, inventory, shipping, regions, product volume and required integrations.

Can a site be moved to another platform later?

Export and migration options differ by platform. Before committing, confirm how pages, posts, products, customers, orders, images and domains can be transferred.
