Squarespace Review: Trial, Domains, Commerce and Export Limits

This review organizes Squarespace’s trial, custom domains, managed hosting, SSL, commerce, transaction fees and content-export scope using official documentation only. It does not independently score template design.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 10 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial Team

Method:We reviewed official pricing, help, security and migration documentation on July 16, 2026. Plan names, prices, availability and features can change, so reconfirm them at checkout.

Scope:This is an official-source review for purchase decisions. We did not perform a reproducible usability or performance test, so it does not rank ease of use, speed or design quality.

Conclusion: a candidate for design-led business sites with built-in selling

Decision summary

Squarespace

Squarespace starts with a 14-day trial rather than a permanent free plan, and paid subscriptions include managed cloud hosting. Products and services can be sold on paid plans, but commerce features and Squarespace transaction fees vary by plan. Migration is based on supported content exports rather than a complete portable copy of the site.

Advantages confirmed in official documentation

  • Evaluate the editor and structure in a 14-day trial without a credit card
  • Managed cloud hosting is included with paid website plans
  • Eligible annual plans include one custom domain for the first year
  • Products, services and gated content can be managed on the same site

Constraints to check before subscribing

  • There is no permanent free plan
  • Sales can involve both payment-processing fees and plan-dependent Squarespace transaction fees
  • Plan and commerce availability should be confirmed for the account and region
  • Not every site feature can be exported and recreated elsewhere

Exports are available, but they are not a complete portable copy of the website

Supported exports

Squarespace documents an XML export for supported pages and blog content, primarily for import into WordPress. Products and contacts have separate import/export workflows.

Limitations

Not every page type, style, custom code, product type, membership feature or integration can be reproduced through the export. Content cannot be exported from a trial site.

Operational note:Before launch, confirm how pages, posts, images, products, customers, orders, form responses and the domain can be recovered or transferred.

Who it may suit and who should compare alternatives

May suit

  • You want a unified design for site and selling
  • Hosting and SSL should be managed by the platform
  • A small team will update a template-based site
  • Built-in memberships, content or scheduling are under consideration

Compare alternatives when

  • A permanent free site is required
  • A complete export to another host is mandatory
  • The project needs a broad plugin ecosystem or custom back end
  • Commerce fees or payment-region support do not meet requirements

Pre-purchase checklist

  1. 1. Use the 14-day trial to test required pages and mobile layouts
  2. 2. Compare monthly and annual billing and the domain renewal after year one
  3. 3. For selling, calculate payment-processing and Squarespace transaction fees separately
  4. 4. Design contributor roles and permissions
  5. 5. Confirm that required pages, products, customers and images can be exported

Frequently asked questions

Does Squarespace have a free plan?

No permanent free plan is offered. Every website can begin with a 14-day trial.

Can I use a custom domain and SSL?

A custom domain can be connected or registered. Correctly connected domains pointing to a Squarespace site receive automatic free SSL.

Can a Squarespace site be moved elsewhere?

Supported content can be exported, but the complete design, functionality and integrations cannot necessarily be recreated on another platform.
