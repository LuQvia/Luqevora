NordPass Review: Teams, Business and Enterprise seats, sharing and SSO

This review separates NordPass’s three business tiers into the fixed 10-user package, the organizational-control tier, and the advanced IdP and SIEM tier using official information only.

Published and reviewed: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora.com Editorial Team

Research method:We reviewed the provider’s official pricing, product, help, and administration documentation on July 16, 2026. Pricing, features, minimum seats, and regional availability can change, so verify the official purchase flow immediately before contracting.

Scope of review:This review organizes official information for purchasing decisions. We did not conduct independent penetration tests, long-term operational testing, or support-response benchmarking, so we do not independently rank security performance or usability.

Advertising disclosure:This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be disclosed near the link and handled under our Affiliate Disclosure. Affiliate Disclosure

Conclusion: Teams for a fixed group of 10, Business for group controls, Enterprise for IdP and SIEM integrations

Decision summary

NordPass

The official site lists Teams as a 10-user pack and Business and Enterprise with a five-user minimum. Teams covers core sharing and administration, Business adds group and folder sharing plus password-health and breach monitoring, and Enterprise adds Entra ID, AD FS, Okta, provisioning, and Sentinel/Splunk integrations. Public prices are dynamically displayed by term and region, so verify checkout.

Advantages confirmed in official information

  • Clear separation between Teams for small groups and Business for scaling organizations
  • Business lists group/folder sharing, password health and breach monitoring
  • Enterprise lists multiple IdPs, provisioning and SIEM integrations
  • Official documentation describes XChaCha20 encryption and zero-knowledge architecture

Constraints to verify before purchase

  • Public prices are dynamic, so term, region and tax must be verified
  • Teams is sold as a 10-user pack even when fewer people use it
  • The official FAQ states that Business cannot be downgraded to Teams
  • Advanced integrations require IdP, SIEM and implementation ownership

Administration and security checks

Total cost of the 10-user pack

Teams is a 10-user package even when fewer people are active. Calculate the full contract total rather than relying only on a per-user display.

Upgrade and downgrade rules

The official FAQ allows Teams-to-Business upgrades but states that Business cannot be downgraded to Teams. Include future organizational structure in the decision.

Before deployment: when using IdP, SCIM or SIEM integrations, test the eligible plan, encryption service, administrator permissions and automated offboarding.

Organizations that may fit—and those that should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • Teams of up to 10 that can use a fixed package
  • Small organizations that want Google Workspace SSO
  • Companies that may later need groups and breach monitoring
  • Organizations planning Entra ID, Okta or SIEM integration

Should compare alternatives

  • Very small teams for which a 10-user pack is inefficient
  • Buyers requiring a fixed public price for immediate comparison
  • Organizations likely to downgrade from Business later
  • Teams that require self-hosting

Frequently asked questions

Does NordPass offer a free business trial?

Yes. The official site offers a 14-day Business trial with no credit card required.

How many users is the Teams plan for?

The official pricing page lists a 10-user pack only, and its FAQ describes Teams as designed for small teams of up to 10 users.

Which identity integrations are listed for Enterprise?

The official plan table lists SSO with Microsoft Entra ID, Microsoft AD FS, and Okta, plus automatic user access management through Entra ID and Okta.
