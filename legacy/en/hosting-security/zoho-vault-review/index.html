Zoho Vault Review: Free, Standard, Professional and Enterprise sharing and administration

This review separates Zoho Vault’s four tiers into personal/basic storage, team sharing, group and emergency controls, and advanced directory, SSO and approval workflows.

Published and reviewed: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora.com Editorial Team

Research method:We reviewed the provider’s official pricing, product, help, and administration documentation on July 16, 2026. Pricing, features, minimum seats, and regional availability can change, so verify the official purchase flow immediately before contracting.

Scope of review:This review organizes official information for purchasing decisions. We did not conduct independent penetration tests, long-term operational testing, or support-response benchmarking, so we do not independently rank security performance or usability.

Conclusion: start with Free storage, use Standard for sharing, Professional for emergency access, and Enterprise for SSO and SIEM

Decision summary

Zoho Vault

Free lists unlimited passwords, passkeys, storage and devices, but organizational sharing and centralized administration begin with Standard. Professional has a five-user minimum and adds groups, shared folders, break-glass emergency access and breach alerts. Enterprise also has a five-user minimum and adds AD, cloud-app SSO, approval workflows and SIEM.

  • Standard adds secure team sharing, ownership transfer and centralized administration

Constraints to verify before purchase

Administration and security checks

Administrator visibility

The official site states that administrators cannot view business passwords unless access is granted and that personal passwords remain inaccessible to everyone, including administrators.

Emergency access and disaster recovery

Separate the Professional break-glass account and the encrypted backup/disaster-recovery configuration documented in the administrator guide from routine administrator permissions.

Before deployment: avoid concentrating Super Admin, emergency access, request-release approval and audit-log roles in one person, and document the recovery procedure.

Organizations that may fit—and those that should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • Organizations that want to start with free storage and expand to sharing
  • Companies using Zoho products, Google Workspace or Microsoft 365
  • Teams requiring group sharing and break-glass emergency access
  • Organizations integrating SSO, SIEM, help desks and approval workflows

Should compare alternatives

  • Teams of one to four that need Professional or Enterprise functions
  • Buyers who need a single fixed public price for immediate comparison
  • Organizations prioritizing a minimal configuration surface
  • Teams that require self-hosting

Frequently asked questions

Does Zoho Vault have a free plan?

Yes. The official pricing page describes Free as free forever with unlimited passwords, passkeys, storage and devices. Team sharing and centralized administration begin with Standard.

Which plan is included in the free trial?

The 15-day trial provides Enterprise features for up to five users. If no paid upgrade is selected, the account moves to Free with its data retained.

What is the minimum seat count for Professional and Enterprise?

The official pricing page states a five-user minimum for Professional and Enterprise.
