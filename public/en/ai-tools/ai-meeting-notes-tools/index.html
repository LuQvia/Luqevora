5 AI Meeting Note and Transcription Tools Compared: Free Plans, Platforms and Data Protection

This comparison covers Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, Notta, tl;dv and Fathom using current official information on free plans, supported meeting platforms, transcription and summary features, storage and AI-training practices. It is not a ranking of transcription accuracy.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 13 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial

Method: We checked each provider's official pricing, feature, help and security pages on July 16, 2026. Prices, free allowances and language support can change. Confirm the final terms on the provider's checkout or contract page.

Limit: Transcript output varies with microphones, speakers, accents, terminology, language and meeting conditions. We do not claim that one product is universally the most accurate.

What we compared

  • Meeting coverage: Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, in-person recording and file import.
  • Free limits: transcription time, storage, summary allowances, meeting limits and downloads.
  • Capture method: visible meeting bot, auto-join, browser extension, desktop and mobile capture.
  • Sharing and integrations: search, CRM, Slack, task systems and calendars.
  • Data controls: storage location, encryption, AI training, retention, deletion and administration.

Product details

1. Otter.ai: meeting capture and AI workflows

Otter's official pricing page includes Zoom, Microsoft Teams and Google Meet, live transcription, speaker identification and 300 monthly minutes in the free Basic plan. Pro adds 1,200 in-app recording minutes and advanced export/search; Business expands meeting duration, imports and administration.

Verified strengths

  • AI Chat during and across meetings
  • Desktop, Chrome and mobile capture options
  • Business activity logs and usage analytics

Check before adopting

  • Basic is limited to 300 minutes/month
  • Pro allows up to 90 minutes/meeting; Business up to four hours
  • Official help identifies US West as the storage region

2. Fireflies.ai: languages and workflow integrations

Fireflies records meetings across Zoom, Google Meet, Teams and other services and advertises transcription in more than 100 languages. Free includes unlimited transcription and AI summaries, but storage is limited to 400 minutes per team.

Verified strengths

  • 100+ transcription languages
  • Unlimited transcription and AI summaries on Free
  • Downloads and integrations on Pro; analytics on Business

Check before adopting

  • Free storage capacity
  • Video and advanced admin plan requirements
  • Plan requirements for HIPAA and private storage

3. Notta: Japanese pricing and mixed recording sources

Notta's Japan pricing page lists 120 monthly minutes on Free, 1,800 minutes on Premium and unlimited transcription on Business. It supports Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, Webex, file imports, Chrome capture and screen recording.

Verified strengths

  • JPY pricing including tax
  • Transcription, translation and custom vocabulary
  • Web meetings, recordings and imported files in one service

Check before adopting

  • Three-minute limit per Free recording
  • AI-summary allowances by plan
  • Plan requirements for no-AI-training controls, SSO and logs

Fathom's official help states that its individual Free plan includes unlimited recording, storage and transcription in 38 languages. Free includes five advanced summaries each month; Premium adds AI action items, follow-up emails, custom summaries and Ask Fathom.

Four operating rules to set before recording

  1. Participant notice: explain the purpose and sharing scope before recording, transcription or AI summaries begin.
  2. Sensitive meetings: define separate handling for customer data, interviews, health, legal, HR and other sensitive discussions.
  3. Sharing controls: minimize automatic sharing, public links, organization-wide access and CRM synchronization.
  4. Human review: verify decisions, owners, deadlines, figures and names before treating the output as the official record.

This is general operational guidance, not legal advice. Check the laws, contracts and internal policies that apply.

Practical selection for a small business

Test real meeting volume on a free plan

For low meeting volume, use the free plans to test meeting entry, capture, transcription and sharing. Free plans differ materially in storage and AI-summary allowances even when transcription is described as unlimited.

Include in-person audio and imported files

If your workflow includes recordings outside web meetings, imported audio/video, custom vocabulary and a Japan-facing contract, review Notta's current conditions first.

Sync sales and customer meetings to a CRM

Fireflies, tl;dv, Fathom and Otter advertise CRM or automation integrations. Compare supported systems, plan requirements, synchronized fields and access permissions.

Handle confidential meetings

Review storage region, retention, deletion, AI training, administrator logs, SSO and default sharing—not only encryption and certifications.

Frequently asked questions

Can a free plan be used for business meetings?

Free plans can be suitable for trials and low-volume use, but storage, AI summaries, downloads, administration and retention controls vary by product and plan. Review the current limits before relying on a free plan.

Can an AI summary be used as the final meeting record?

It should be reviewed by a person. Confirm speakers, figures, decisions, owners and deadlines, and obtain participant approval where your process requires it.

Should participants be informed before recording?

Inform participants before recording or transcription begins and check the laws, contracts, internal policies and meeting-platform settings that apply to the meeting.
