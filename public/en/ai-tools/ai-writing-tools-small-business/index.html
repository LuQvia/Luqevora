5 AI Writing Tools for Small Businesses: Pricing, Features and Data Policies

This guide compares ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Notion AI, and Grammarly using official pricing pages, help documentation, and privacy or security statements. It is a workflow-fit comparison—not a claim that one model always produces better writing.

Published and verified: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 12 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial

Method: We checked each vendor’s official pricing, help, privacy, and security materials on July 16, 2026. Taxes, regional pricing, currencies, billing intervals, and promotions can change, so verify the checkout page before purchasing.

Scope: Only reproducible official facts are presented as facts. We do not make unverified claims about which system writes “most naturally,” is “most accurate,” or is universally the “best.”

Commercial disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. Any future affiliate relationship will be disclosed near the relevant link and under our Affiliate Disclosure.

How we compared the tools

  • Document capabilities: Officially documented drafting, summarizing, rewriting, tone, and in-document editing.
  • Workflow integration: Connections to Google Workspace, Notion, company knowledge, browsers, or desktop writing surfaces.
  • Pricing predictability: Free access, individual subscriptions, per-seat billing, minimum seats, and regional variation.
  • Administration and data controls: Training defaults, admin settings, dedicated workspaces, permissions, and identity management.
  • Business fit: Practical fit for a solo operator, a small team, or a company already centered on a specific platform.

Official facts and practical fit

1. ChatGPT: broad drafting and research workflows

OpenAI’s official plan comparison documents search, file uploads, projects, data analysis, deep research, and writing-adjacent capabilities. It is a candidate when a business wants to move from source material to an outline, draft, summary, table, or analysis in one service.

Suitable workflows

  • Email, proposal, and article drafts
  • Writing based on PDFs, spreadsheets, and presentations
  • Turning research into structured text and tables

Before purchase

  • Individual and Business plans have different controls
  • Business starts at two users
  • Confirm local pricing and tax at checkout

Data: OpenAI states that inputs and outputs from ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, and other business products are not used to train models by default.

2. Claude: long documents and shared knowledge

Anthropic’s official Team documentation lists projects, knowledge bases, workplace connectors, centralized administration and billing, SSO, permissions, and a 200k context window. Team Standard requires at least five members and is listed at US $25 per member monthly or $20 per member per month with annual billing.

Suitable workflows

  • Long reports, policies, and manuals
  • Drafting against project-specific reference material
  • Searching connected Google Drive, Slack, or Microsoft 365 content

Before purchase

  • Pro and Team are separate account types
  • Team requires at least five members
  • Regional currency and tax can differ

Data: Anthropic states that inputs and outputs from commercial products such as Team and Enterprise are not used for model training by default unless the customer explicitly submits feedback or otherwise permits use.

Official individual plan guide / Official Team plan guide

3. Gemini: writing inside Google Workspace

Google Workspace pricing documents Gemini assistance in Gmail on Business Starter and broader integration across Docs, Meet, and other apps on Business Standard and higher. This makes Gemini a practical candidate when email, documents, spreadsheets, meetings, and company accounts are already managed through Workspace.

Suitable workflows

  • Drafting and summarizing email in Gmail
  • Writing and revising in Google Docs
  • Using existing Workspace content in business writing

Pricing view

  • US Workspace list prices: $7/$14/$22 on annual commitment
  • Billing is per user and local prices vary
  • Higher tiers add storage, controls, and broader AI integration

Data: Google states that licensed Workspace with Gemini submissions are not used to train models and are not reviewed by humans.

Official Workspace pricing / Workspace with Gemini data protections

4. Notion AI: writing where company knowledge already lives

Notion documents AI writing and editing, workspace and connected-app search, database creation and autofill, meeting transcription and summaries, and research. The help center states that full Notion AI access is available on Business and Enterprise, while Free and Plus receive limited complimentary responses for evaluation.

Suitable workflows

  • Writing from an internal wiki
  • Keeping meeting notes and tasks together
  • Summarizing and populating database records

Before purchase

  • Billing is per workspace member
  • Free and Plus AI use is limited and trial-oriented
  • AI connectors require Business or Enterprise

Data: Notion states that workspace data and personal information are not used to train its models by default, and AI subprocessors are contractually prohibited from using customer data to train their models.

Official Notion pricing / Notion AI security and privacy

5. Grammarly: English editing and brand consistency

Grammarly Pro documents full-sentence rewrites, tone suggestions, plagiarism detection, generative AI, and team features including style guides, brand tones, knowledge sharing, snippets, and usage analytics. Official pricing is US $30 monthly, $60 quarterly, or $144 annually per member, with support for up to 149 seats.

Suitable workflows

  • Editing English email, proposals, and web copy
  • Standardizing tone and terminology across a team
  • Improving existing text inside writing applications

Important limitation

  • The core role is writing improvement, not broad research
  • Generative AI use has plan limits
  • Review the training-control default for the purchase channel

Data: Grammarly states that Product Improvement and Training is enabled by default for individual and directly purchased Pro accounts but can be turned off. Certain sales-assisted business and enterprise accounts default to off.

Five checks before entering confidential data

  1. Consumer or organization plan: Products with the same name can have materially different administration and data terms.
  2. Training default: “Not used by default” is different from “enabled unless you opt out.”
  3. Prohibited content: Avoid entering passwords, payment details, government identifiers, health records, and unpublished customer information without an approved policy.
  4. Admin controls: Review SSO, offboarding, sharing scopes, connectors, and permissions.
  5. Human review: Verify facts, numbers, legal references, citations, rights, and regulated claims before publishing.

A practical selection path for a small business

One or two people with broad needs

Start with a free or individual ChatGPT or Claude plan for public-information drafting, summarizing, and outlining. When confidential customer or company material becomes part of the workflow, compare the relevant Business or Team terms. Note that Claude Team requires five members, while ChatGPT Business starts at two users.

A Google Workspace-centered company

When email, documents, spreadsheets, and meetings already run on Workspace, integrated Gemini features may reduce copying between systems and separate account administration.

A Notion-centered company

When the internal wiki, meeting notes, projects, and databases already live in Notion, Notion AI can use that existing structure. Full AI access requires Business or Enterprise.

English quality and brand consistency

When the primary requirement is English grammar, tone, reusable wording, and style governance, Grammarly is a specialist candidate rather than a substitute for a broad research assistant.

Frequently asked questions

Can a small business rely only on free plans?

Free plans can support trials and occasional drafting, but usage limits, administration, and data controls differ from paid and business plans. Review a business contract and internal policy before handling confidential information.

Can AI-generated copy be published without review?

No. A person should verify facts, copyright and trademark issues, personal data, potentially misleading claims, and any industry-specific rules before publication.

Which single tool should a small business choose?

It depends on the workflow. ChatGPT or Claude fit broad drafting and research, Gemini fits Google Workspace workflows, Notion AI fits knowledge already stored in Notion, and Grammarly fits English editing and brand consistency.
