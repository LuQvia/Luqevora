5 Project Management Tools for Small Businesses: Free Plans, Pricing and Permissions Compared

This guide compares Notion, Asana, ClickUp, monday.com and Trello using official information on free plans, task and project features, external guests, permissions and upgrade conditions. It is not a subjective productivity ranking.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 16 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial Team

Research method: We reviewed official pricing pages, product pages and permission or user-management documentation on July 16, 2026. Prices vary by currency, tax, billing cycle, region and contract date, so confirm the final amount in the vendor’s checkout.

Audience: Sole proprietors, small businesses and small teams managing tasks, deadlines, owners, projects, documents and external collaborators.

Seven checks before adoption

01

What must be managed

Decide whether you need only tasks or also clients, documents, approvals, time and operational records.

02

Users and billing roles

Identify how members, viewers, guests and external contractors are counted for billing.

03

Required views

List, Kanban, calendar, timeline, Gantt and dashboards should be selected based on daily use.

04

Permissions and privacy

Check admin, editor, viewer, guest, private-project and item-level restrictions.

05

Automations

Confirm run limits, integrations, conditions, notifications and form intake included in each plan.

06

Migration and export

Review CSV import/export and whether attachments, comments and history can be transferred.

07

Administration and security

Check 2FA, SSO, SCIM, audit logs, data residency and offboarding controls.

Official features and contract checks

Notion

Notion combines pages, databases, documents, forms and tasks in one workspace. The Free plan provides unlimited pages and blocks for individual use, while free workspaces with two or more members have a block limit. Free also lists 10 external guests and a 5MB limit per uploaded file.

  • Manage tasks alongside manuals and meeting notes
  • Plus lists unlimited guests and file uploads
  • Business lists SAML SSO, granular database permissions and private teamspaces
  • Enterprise adds SCIM, audit logs and advanced sharing controls

Before purchase: Verify member versus guest roles, teamspace design, page and database permissions, history length, file limits and AI terms.

Asana

Asana centers work around tasks, assignees, dates, projects, dependencies and status reporting. Personal is free for up to two users and lists unlimited tasks and projects, list/board/calendar views and files up to 100MB.

  • Starter lists Timeline/Gantt, dashboards and unlimited automations
  • Paid plans list unlimited members, private teams/projects, guests and admin console
  • Higher tiers expand portfolios, goals, workload and reporting
  • Enterprise lists SAML authentication, SCIM and guest invitation controls

Before purchase: Check free user limits, required views, rules, forms, portfolios, view-only licensing and external guest workflows.

Read the detailed Asana review →

ClickUp

ClickUp combines tasks, Docs, Whiteboards, Forms, Calendar, time tracking, dashboards and Chat. Free Forever provides 60MB of storage while listing unlimited tasks and free-plan members.

  • Unlimited adds unlimited storage, Gantt, forms, integrations, custom fields and native time tracking
  • Business lists advanced dashboards, 5,000 monthly automations and Google SSO
  • Unlimited includes guests with permission control
  • Enterprise adds advanced permissions, custom roles, SAML/SCIM, audit logs and data residency

Before purchase: Confirm workspace-wide upgrade rules, storage, guest seats, automation usage and separate AI credits or plans.

Read the detailed ClickUp review →

monday.com

monday work management uses boards, items and columns to model projects, tasks, clients and operational requests. Its Free plan is limited to two seats, three boards and three Docs, and the company lists a 14-day Pro trial.

  • Paid plan purchases start at three users
  • Features by tier include Timeline/Gantt, Calendar, guests, integrations and automations
  • Pro lists private boards and time tracking
  • Board owners can configure what other users may view or change

Before purchase: Check seat blocks, board types, guests, monthly automation and integration actions, and dashboard board limits.

Read the monday.com review →

Trello

Trello is a Kanban-style service built around boards, lists and cards. Free supports up to ten collaborators and ten boards per Workspace, with unlimited cards and Power-Ups.

  • Free lists 10MB per file, 250 Workspace command runs/month and two-factor authentication
  • Standard adds unlimited boards, custom fields, single-board guests and 250MB per file
  • Premium adds Calendar, Timeline, Table, Dashboard and Map views plus admin features
  • Every member is an admin in a Free Workspace; paid Workspaces can separate roles

Before purchase: Confirm board counts, billing for guests on multiple boards, command limits, required views, admin controls and export options.

How to narrow the options by use case

Combine an internal wiki with task management

Start with Notion and validate database design, external guests, permission boundaries, history and file limits.

Standardize project ownership, deadlines and dependencies

Start with Asana and determine whether dependencies, Timeline, rules, forms, portfolios and status reporting are required.

Consolidate several work functions into one platform

Test ClickUp and monday.com, focusing on whether the configuration remains maintainable and whether permissions, automations and views fit real users.

Start with a simple Kanban workflow

Run one real project on Trello Free, then check whether board, collaborator, admin, Power-Up and command limits are sufficient.

Frequently asked questions

Can a team operate entirely on a free plan?

A small team can test basic task sharing, but free plans limit users, boards, storage, history, permissions or automations. Validate the limits against your real team and workflow.

Are guests billed as paid users?

It depends on the service and plan. Some provide free guests, while multi-board guests or permission-controlled guests may be billable. Check each service’s billing role definitions before purchase.

Should we choose the tool with the most features?

Choose based on the views, fields, notifications, approvals, permissions, external sharing and export options your team will use consistently, not on total feature count.
