Core capabilities and workflow

Both support direct and group conversation, threaded work, files, meetings, apps, and administration, but their ecosystems and information ownership differ.

FactorWhat to decide
EcosystemSlack is vendor-neutral; Google Chat is Workspace-native
FilesSlack links many stores; Google Chat centers Drive
CostCompare standalone Slack with the Workspace edition already owned
  • Slack: channels, app ecosystem, Slack Connect, huddles, and workflow tools
  • Google Chat: Spaces, Drive files, Meet, tasks, and Workspace identity
  • Both: plan-specific retention, export, guests, and security

Strengths and trade-offs

Slack adds a specialized communication layer; Google Chat reduces vendor count but may not match every external integration requirement.

  • Strength: A clear ecosystem-based decision

  • Verify: History, retention, and export by plan
  • Verify: Migration of messages and connected app workflows

Who it suits

Use the organization's file store and identity system as the first filter.

  • Slack: multi-vendor SaaS environments
  • Google Chat: Workspace-standardized teams
  • Organizations ready to govern conversation and files

Implementation checklist

Pilot Slack and Google Chat in a small scope and document cost, workflow, migration, and administration before rollout.

  • Define the objective and completion criteria in one sentence
  • Reproduce a representative task in a free plan or trial
  • Test administrator, contributor, and viewer permissions
  • Perform a real backup or export test
  • Record the renewal date and internal owner
