Verdict: a clear option for unified POS and payments

Square fits small businesses that want POS, payments, hardware, online selling, and invoices under one account. Published hardware prices and major processing rates make an initial budget easier to build.

Advanced restaurant, retail, staff, and kitchen functions may require paid plans. Test whether the free standard POS reproduces the required workflow before adding modules.

Software pricing and hardware

The standard Square POS application has no registration or monthly fee. Official pricing lists Square Reader at JPY 4,980, Square Stand at JPY 29,980, and Square Terminal at JPY 39,980.

Initial cost differs between a configuration using existing phones or tablets and one using dedicated Square hardware. Include receipt printing, customer display, portability, battery life, and counter space.

Processing fees

Eligible Japanese small businesses with annual cashless volume below JPY 30 million can receive 2.5% processing for named major in-person card brands. Other transactions use 3.25% or higher rates or a custom arrangement.

Online payments are listed at 3.6%, card-not-present entry at 3.75%, and invoices at 3.25% in relevant workflows. Model in-person and remote revenue separately.

Core functions and expansion

Square combines catalog, inventory, customers, reports, staff access, payments, and refunds. It can expand into Square Online, invoices, appointments, and industry-specific POS products.

A business that wants store and online catalog, customer, and payment data in one ecosystem may benefit. Existing ecommerce and accounting systems require a clear source-of-truth design.

Advantages and cautions

A business can test the free POS and choose hardware without a software subscription. Settlement is documented as next-business-day for two named banks and weekly Friday deposits for other supported banks.

The 2.5% rate is conditional and not universal across every merchant or method. Advanced restaurant and retail functions can create additional software cost.

Pre-launch checklist

Test catalog setup, checkout, returns, inventory, customers, and reports in a free account, then select hardware. If online selling or invoices are required, confirm channel-specific fees, settlement, and accounting integration.

Select hardware only after mapping the counter layout, mobility needs, receipt workflow, network conditions, and peak transaction volume. Run a pilot with refunds, discounts, split payments, offline contingencies, inventory adjustments, and report exports. The standard POS may have no monthly software fee, but the complete operating design includes hardware, processing, accessories, and staff procedures.
