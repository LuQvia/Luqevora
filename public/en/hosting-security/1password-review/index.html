1Password Review: Teams Starter Pack and Business Pricing, Sharing and Admin Controls

We review 1Password business plans by separating the fixed-price package for teams of up to ten from the per-user Business plan, using current official pricing, administration, and security documentation.

Published and reviewed: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial

Research method: We reviewed the provider's official pricing, product, security, and administration documentation on July 16, 2026. Pricing, features, minimum seats, and regional availability can change, so verify the final checkout or sales terms before purchasing.

Review scope: This article organizes official information for procurement decisions. We did not conduct independent penetration tests, long-term operational testing, or support-response benchmarking, so we do not independently rank security performance or usability.

Advertising disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be disclosed near the link and handled under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Conclusion: a fixed package for up to ten users, then per-user Business pricing as the team grows

Decision summary

1Password

At review, Teams Starter Pack costs $24.95 per month for up to 10 users when billed annually. Business costs $8.99 per user per month when billed annually. Both offer a 14-day trial, and official materials describe shared vaults, role-based permissions, Watchtower, and onboarding and offboarding controls.

Advantages confirmed in official information

  • A fixed-price package for teams of up to ten
  • Shared vaults and role-based permissions
  • Watchtower alerts for weak, reused, or exposed credentials
  • Secret Key plus account password, AES-256 end-to-end encryption, and a zero-knowledge architecture

Constraints to verify before purchase

  • No permanent free plan; a paid subscription is required after the trial
  • The displayed Teams monthly price is tied to annual billing
  • Teams above ten users must recalculate costs under Business or another plan
  • Advanced enterprise products and capabilities may require a quote or separate purchase

Security architecture

  • AES-256 end-to-end encryption
  • Zero-knowledge architecture
  • Account password plus Secret Key
  • Third-party reviews and a bug-bounty program

Onboarding, offboarding, and least-privilege design

Small teams

Teams Starter Pack covers up to ten users at a fixed package price. Shared vaults and role-based permissions should be configured so employees receive only the credentials required for their roles.

Growing organizations

Business adds identity-provider integrations and expanded administration. Define hiring, departure, group, vault-ownership, and audit procedures before rollout.

Important: Document administrator recovery, Secret Key custody, and emergency procedures before production use.

Organizations that may fit—and those that should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • Teams of up to ten that want a predictable package price
  • Organizations introducing shared vaults and role-based access
  • Teams managing passwords and passkeys in one product
  • Organizations that may later add IdP integrations

Should compare alternatives

  • Organizations requiring a permanent free business tier
  • Teams that require self-hosting
  • Headcounts that frequently move above and below ten, making a fixed package inefficient
  • Organizations seeking privileged-access management in the same base subscription

Frequently asked questions

How much is Teams Starter Pack?

The official page displayed $24.95 per month for up to ten users, billed annually, on July 16, 2026. Regional displays and future prices may differ, so verify checkout.

Does 1Password offer a free version?

1Password advertises a 14-day free trial rather than a permanent free plan. A subscription is required after the trial.

What is the main difference between Teams Starter Pack and Business?

Starter is a fixed package for up to ten users. Business is priced per user and the official page lists integrations with Okta, Entra ID, OneLogin, Duo, and additional administration capabilities.
