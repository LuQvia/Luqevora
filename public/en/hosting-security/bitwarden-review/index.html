Bitwarden Review: Teams and Enterprise Pricing, Sharing, SCIM and Self-Hosting

We compare Bitwarden Teams and Enterprise using official pricing and documentation for credential sharing, audit events, provisioning, SSO, account recovery, and self-hosted deployment.

Published and reviewed: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 11 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial

Research method: We reviewed the provider's official pricing, product, security, and administration documentation on July 16, 2026. Pricing, features, minimum seats, and regional availability can change, so verify the final checkout or sales terms before purchasing.

Review scope: This article organizes official information for procurement decisions. We did not conduct independent penetration tests, long-term operational testing, or support-response benchmarking, so we do not independently rank security performance or usability.

Advertising disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be disclosed near the link and handled under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Conclusion: SCIM starts with Teams; Enterprise adds SSO, recovery, and self-hosting

Decision summary

Bitwarden

Teams costs $4 per user per month when billed annually, while Enterprise costs $6. Teams includes secure sharing, event logs, directory synchronization, and SCIM. Enterprise adds granular access control, passwordless SSO, account recovery, self-hosting flexibility, and other enterprise capabilities.

Advantages confirmed in official information

  • Event logs, directory synchronization, and SCIM in Teams
  • Passwordless SSO and account recovery in Enterprise
  • Self-hosting flexibility in Enterprise
  • Open-source security, zero-knowledge encryption, and encrypted exports

Constraints to verify before purchase

  • USD pricing is based on annual billing and excludes taxes
  • SSO, recovery, and self-hosting are Enterprise capabilities
  • Self-hosting transfers upgrades, availability, and backup responsibilities to the customer
  • Advanced remediation and large-deployment services may require qualification or a quote

Cloud and self-hosted operational responsibility

Cloud deployment

Even when using the hosted service, customers must design administrator roles, Collections, Groups, export controls, and recovery policies.

Self-hosting

Enterprise advertises self-hosting flexibility. The customer becomes responsible for server updates, TLS, backups, monitoring, incident response, and network exposure.

Procurement point: Self-hosting can provide deployment control, but it does not reduce operational responsibility.

Organizations that may fit—and those that should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • Organizations wanting per-user pricing from a low entry point
  • Teams that need SCIM and event logs before moving to Enterprise
  • Buyers that include open-source software in their evaluation criteria
  • Organizations that may later require self-hosting

Should compare alternatives

  • Organizations that require short-term monthly billing instead of annual billing
  • Teams without staff to maintain a self-hosted service
  • Small teams for which a fixed ten-user package is cheaper
  • Organizations seeking full PAM and secrets management in the base product

Frequently asked questions

How much is Bitwarden Teams?

The official business pricing page lists Teams at $4 per user per month, billed annually, before tax. Enterprise is $6 per user per month.

Is SCIM available in Teams?

The official pricing page lists directory synchronization and SCIM under Teams. Verify the identity-provider setup and current implementation limits before deployment.

Which plan supports self-hosting?

The official pricing page lists self-hosting flexibility under Enterprise. Customers are responsible for infrastructure maintenance, updates, and backups.
