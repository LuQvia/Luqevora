CPI Rental Server Review for Businesses in Japan

CPI Business Standard is a Japan-focused shared hosting service for organizations that need business email, separated multi-domain environments, staging, release controls, and documented recovery procedures. It is not primarily a low-cost personal blogging plan.

Conclusion: a fit for business email and controlled multi-site operations

CPI is best evaluated as an operational package for corporate websites and email rather than as a budget hosting plan. The official specification lists 300 GB of SSD storage for the primary web domain and up to ten multi-domain environments, each with a separate 100 GB web area.

Its differentiators are SmartRelease staging and deployment, separated web and mail infrastructure, SPF, DKIM and DMARC support, and a defined SLA scope. Buyers should distinguish standard features from paid options before signing a contract.

Pricing and contract terms

The official pricing page lists three, six, and twelve-month terms. At the verification date, the twelve-month term was displayed at JPY 4,840 per month equivalent, JPY 58,080 in total, with no setup fee. Shorter terms had a higher monthly equivalent and a setup fee.

Website operations and SmartRelease

SmartRelease combines a test environment, controlled production release, backup, and recovery workflows. This is useful when a company needs a review step before publishing changes or when an agency manages client websites.

The standard SmartRelease backup is limited to a combined 10 GB of web files and databases for up to 30 generations. The 300 GB primary web capacity does not mean that the entire capacity is retained for 30 generations.

Email, security and SLA scope

The mail service supports SPF, DKIM and DMARC, along with webmail, forwarding, autoresponders and mailing lists. WAF and a CPI SSL certificate are included, while website tamper detection is listed as a paid option.

The SLA uses a 100 percent service-level reference for the primary web server. The official specification excludes the mail server and servers used for multi-domain environments from that SLA scope.

Support and migration considerations

Standard support is email-based. The 24-hour telephone and email service is a paid option. Migration assistance may also involve paid work, so the organization should document who handles data transfer, testing, DNS changes, and rollback.

CPI also publishes trial and refund conditions. Review exclusions, deadlines, and domain-related charges directly in the current policy before relying on a refund option.

Who should consider CPI

CPI is relevant to Japanese companies, organizations, and web agencies that value business email, multi-domain isolation, staging, and recovery procedures. A low-cost shared host may be better for one small personal site, while a VPS or managed dedicated service may be better when root access or isolated compute resources are required.

Frequently asked questions

What was the displayed twelve-month CPI price at verification?

The official page displayed JPY 4,840 per month equivalent, JPY 58,080 total, and no setup fee for the twelve-month term. Recheck the checkout page before purchase.

Is 24-hour phone support included by default?

No. Standard support is email-based, and the 24-hour telephone and email service is listed as a paid option.

Does the 30-generation backup cover all 300 GB?

No. The standard SmartRelease backup is described as a combined 10 GB for web files and databases, retained for up to 30 generations.

Does the CPI SLA cover mail and all multi-domain servers?

No. The published scope applies to the primary web server and excludes the mail server and multi-domain servers.
