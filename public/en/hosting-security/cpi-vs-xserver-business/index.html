CPI vs XServer Business for Organizations in Japan

CPI emphasizes SmartRelease, separated domain environments, and business email operations. XServer Business emphasizes capacity, fourteen-day remote backups, migration assistance, and administrative controls.

Pricing and total cost

CPI displayed JPY 4,840 per month equivalent for a twelve-month contract with no setup fee. XServer Business displayed JPY 4,180 per month for the twelve-month shared Standard plan plus a JPY 16,500 setup fee. A temporary cashback campaign was visible on XServer Business at verification.

Compare renewal pricing, setup fees, migration work, backup requirements, domains, and optional support rather than using one promotional number.

Backup and publishing operations

CPI SmartRelease combines staging, production release, and recovery. Its standard backup allowance is a combined 10 GB for web and database data, retained for up to 30 generations.

XServer Business states that web files, mail data, and MySQL databases are kept for the previous 14 days and stored remotely. Confirm recovery granularity and operational procedures before purchase.

Email, administration and support

CPI publishes SPF, DKIM, DMARC, webmail, forwarding, and autoresponder support. Standard support is email-based, while the 24-hour telephone service is a paid option.

XServer Business includes telephone, email, and chat support and provides administrator-user controls. Some advanced multi-account and dedicated-IP features depend on the selected plan.

How to choose

Document the publishing process, support responsibilities, data recovery objectives, and required administrator roles before selecting a service.

  • CPI fit: agency or internal team needs staging and release control
  • CPI fit: multiple domains and Japanese business email need separated environments
  • XServer Business fit: large storage, migration support and standard multi-channel support
  • XServer Business fit: administrator controls and corporate domain benefits are important

Frequently asked questions

Which service had the lower twelve-month monthly price at verification?

XServer Business shared Standard displayed JPY 4,180 per month, while CPI displayed JPY 4,840 per month equivalent. Setup fees and campaign conditions make total cost different.

Which backup model is broader?

CPI provides up to 30 generations within a combined 10 GB web and database allowance. XServer Business publishes fourteen days of web, mail and MySQL backups with remote storage. Choose based on data scope and recovery needs.

Which is better for a web agency?

CPI is attractive for staging and controlled releases through SmartRelease. XServer Business is attractive when migration assistance, capacity, and administrative controls are more important.
