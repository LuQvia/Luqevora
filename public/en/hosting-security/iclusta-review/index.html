Plans and capacity

The official feature table lists maximum SSD capacity of 300GB for Mini, 500GB for Regular, and 750GB for Pro. Domain, concurrent-access, and scale-up limits vary by plan.

SLA, SSL, and backup

The provider describes a 100% SLA covering web, mail, and database servers, with service credits based on measured availability. This is a credit framework, not a promise of zero downtime. AlphaSSL is included across all plans.

WordPress and Movable Type

WordPress auto-installation, LiteSpeed Cache support, and paid setup or migration assistance are available. Regular and Pro include one commercial Movable Type user license, while broader licensing and maintenance cost extra.

Contract caveats

Review the surcharge, domain charges, support hours, recovery fees, and refund exclusions before ordering. The 30-day refund program excludes some costs, including domains and later add-ons.

Pricing and invoice review

The published plan fee is not the only cost to evaluate. iCLUSTA+ states that a separate surcharge applies to Mini, Regular, and Pro. Buyers should save the quotation or checkout total and identify domain registration, SSL upgrades, setup assistance, restore work, and other optional charges before approval.

The A8 program requires at least two months of confirmed use for a fixed affiliate commission. That condition is relevant to the publisher, while customers should separately evaluate the provider’s own minimum terms, refund exclusions, and cancellation workflow.

Email and domain operations

Unlimited email-address creation does not remove operational limits. Administrators should estimate mailbox storage, retention, forwarding, spam filtering, authentication records, and the time needed to migrate existing users. Test sending and receiving with the organization’s actual DNS configuration before moving production mail.

The official feature table also publishes plan-specific domain and capacity limits. A business with many brands or client sites should map each domain, database, certificate, and mailbox to the selected plan rather than relying on the headline maximum alone.

Support and recovery planning

Phone and email support can be valuable for organizations without a dedicated server administrator, but support hours and the boundary between guidance and paid work must be documented. Confirm who changes DNS, imports databases, validates forms, and restores mail after an incident.

The standard backup statement should not be treated as a complete disaster-recovery plan. Ask what is retained, how long it is retained, who can request a restore, how much restoration costs, and how long recovery normally takes. Keep an independent copy of critical website and database data.
