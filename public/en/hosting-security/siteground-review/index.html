SiteGround Review: StartUp, GrowBig and GoGeek Introductory, Renewal and Backup Terms

This review checks SiteGround’s introductory and renewal pricing, sites, storage, daily backups, staging and WordPress operations using official materials.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time:11 minBy Luqevora Editorial

Research method: We checked the provider’s official pricing, help, security, backup and migration materials on July 16, 2026. Pricing, promotions, renewal rates, limits and regional availability can change; verify the checkout terms before purchasing.

Scope: This review organizes official information for purchase decisions. We did not run a reproducible speed benchmark or long-term support test, so we do not independently rank performance, uptime or support quality.

Affiliate disclosure: This article contains no affiliate links at publication. If links are added later, they will be identified near the link and under our Affiliate Disclosure.

Conclusion: a bundled daily-backup and WordPress operations option, with renewal increases to budget

Decision summary

SiteGround

SiteGround provides web hosting and WordPress management through Site Tools. It lists SSL, CDN, daily backups, email, migration and 24/7 support across plans. The promotional rates are prepaid for 12 months, so renewal cost must be included in the decision.

Advantages confirmed from official information

  • Free SSL, CDN, daily backups and email are listed on all plans
  • WordPress migration, managed updates, WP-CLI and SSH are listed
  • GrowBig and higher include on-demand backups and staging
  • GoGeek adds Git, Private DNS, white-label access and priority support

Constraints to verify before buying

  • Introductory and renewal prices differ substantially
  • Displayed offers are prepaid for 12 months and exclude VAT
  • Site, storage and advanced features vary by plan
  • “Unlimited traffic” still requires checking actual resource conditions

Daily backups with 30-day history; on-demand backups on higher plans

Automatic backups

SiteGround states that it backs up the entire site daily and keeps 30 days of history. Verify the restore scope and workflow after activation.

GrowBig and GoGeek

Both higher plans list on-demand backups. GoGeek also lists staging plus Git.

Cost note: Compare the second-year total using renewal pricing and VAT—not only the introductory rate.

Who may be a fit—and who should compare alternatives

May be a fit

  • You want daily backup, SSL, CDN and email bundled
  • You need WordPress updates, migration and staging
  • You manage multiple sites on GrowBig or higher
  • You want separate site access for collaborators

Compare alternatives when

  • You want flat pricing from introductory through renewal
  • You only want the lowest long-term hosting price
  • You require root access or a custom server stack
  • You want a specialist visit-based managed WordPress service

Pre-purchase checklist

  2. 2. Calculate first-term and renewal totals
  3. 3. Confirm sites, storage and expected traffic
  4. 4. Decide whether on-demand backup, staging and Git are required
  5. 5. Check VAT, domain renewal, email and migration terms

Frequently asked questions

Does the $2.99 StartUp rate continue at renewal?

No. The official page describes it as a 12-month prepaid introductory rate and states renewal at $17.99/month excluding VAT.

Why consider GrowBig instead of StartUp?

At the time checked, GrowBig adds unlimited sites, 50GB storage, on-demand backups and staging.

How long are automatic backups kept?

SiteGround states that it creates daily backups and keeps 30 days of backup history.
