5 Web Hosting Services for Small Businesses: Pricing, Backups and Management Compared

Hosting decisions should account for renewal pricing, backups, migration, WordPress operations, support and overage charges—not only the introductory price. This article compares five services using official information.

Comparison scope

Hostinger and SiteGround are general web hosting services, Kinsta and WP Engine are managed WordPress platforms, and Cloudways is managed cloud hosting. They are not identical products, so compare the management model and required capabilities rather than treating the table as a simple price ranking.

Practical shortlist by use case

Start with a low introductory cost

Hostinger combines long-term introductory pricing with domain, SSL, backups and migration features. Check the prepaid term and renewal rate before purchasing.

Use shared hosting with integrated operations

SiteGround includes daily backups, SSL, CDN, email and WordPress updates on all plans, with staging and on-demand backups on higher tiers.

Delegate more WordPress operations

Kinsta and WP Engine focus on WordPress backups, staging, security and specialist support. Their entry prices are higher than general shared hosting.

Use scalable cloud infrastructure

Cloudways supports managed servers on DigitalOcean, AWS and Google Cloud with hourly billing. Review bandwidth and infrastructure-specific charges.

Official details and contract checks

Hostinger

Hostinger combines long-term introductory pricing with WordPress, a website builder, domains, SSL and migration. The Premium example is $2.99/mo on a 48-month term and renews at $10.99/mo.

  • Premium: 3 websites, 20 GB SSD and weekly backups
  • Unlimited: unlimited websites, 50 GB NVMe and daily backups
  • A free first-year domain is listed with annual plans
  • 30-day money-back guarantee, 24/7 support and automated migration

Before buying: confirm the prepaid term, renewal price, backup schedule and the free period for email and domain features.

Read the detailed Hostinger review

SiteGround

SiteGround combines web hosting and WordPress management in Site Tools. StartUp supports one site and 10 GB, while GrowBig supports multiple sites and 50 GB and adds staging and on-demand backups.

  • Daily backups, SSL, CDN and email on all plans
  • WordPress migration, managed updates, WP-CLI and SSH
  • StartUp introductory example: $2.99/mo; renewal: $17.99/mo
  • 30-day money-back guarantee and 24/7 support

Before buying: confirm renewal pricing, storage, estimated visits, backup downloads and VAT.

Read the detailed SiteGround review

Kinsta

Kinsta is managed WordPress cloud hosting. Single 20GB includes one site, 20GB server bandwidth and 10GB storage for $35 monthly or $350 billed annually.

  • Daily backups, staging, APM and CDN
  • WAF, DDoS protection and malware remediation support
  • Unlimited users with access controls and 24/7 support
  • A downloadable backup can be generated weekly

Before buying: confirm the visit/bandwidth metric, retention, overage charges and paid hourly or external backup options.

Read the detailed Kinsta review

Cloudways

Cloudways adds application management, backups, SSL and monitoring to selected cloud infrastructure. A 1GB DigitalOcean server is listed from $11/month or $0.0153/hour.

  • Hourly and monthly usage-based billing with vertical scaling
  • Backup frequency from one hour to seven days and retention from one to four weeks
  • Free SSL, staging, SSH/SFTP and 24/7 monitoring
  • Off-site backups listed at $0.033 per GB

Before buying: confirm provider, region, bandwidth overages, backup storage, separate email and the absence of root access.

Read the detailed Cloudways review

WP Engine

WP Engine is managed WordPress hosting. Startup is listed from $30/month with one site, an estimated 25,000 visits, 10GB storage and 75GB bandwidth.

  • Daily and on-demand backups, SSL and SSH
  • Security patching, plugin risk scans and DDoS protection
  • Staging and development environments plus GitHub Actions deployment
  • 24/7 WordPress expertise and a free migration plugin

Before buying: confirm first-year terms, renewal, visit counting, bandwidth, storage, extra sites and paid extensions.

Frequently asked questions

Should I choose based on the lowest introductory price?

No. Introductory and renewal prices may differ substantially. Compare contract length, renewal price, taxes, overage charges, migration and backup costs.

What is the difference between shared hosting and managed WordPress hosting?

Shared hosting generally offers lower-cost support for multiple site types. Managed WordPress hosting focuses on WordPress-specific updates, caching, staging, security and specialist support.

Is the host’s backup enough on its own?

For important sites, also confirm whether backups can be downloaded or copied to external storage rather than relying only on restore points held by the same hosting provider.
