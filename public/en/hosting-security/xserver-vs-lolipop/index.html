Strengths and trade-offs

The choice is partly between starting at a smaller price and paying for more capacity early, with future migration cost included.

  • Strength: Japan-focused information and support
  • Strength: Clear WordPress launch options
  • Verify: Do not compare the cheapest tier to the required tier
  • Verify: Renewal, restoration, and domain benefits

Who it suits

Use budget, expected site count in three years, and operating owner to decide.

  • XServer: multiple sites and longer-term operations
  • Lolipop: small, lower-cost launch
  • Beginners: a tier with the needed recovery and support

Implementation checklist

Pilot XServer and Lolipop in a small scope and document cost, workflow, migration, and administration before rollout.

  • Define the objective and completion criteria in one sentence
  • Reproduce a representative task in a free plan or trial
  • Test administrator, contributor, and viewer permissions
  • Perform a real backup or export test
  • Record the renewal date and internal owner
