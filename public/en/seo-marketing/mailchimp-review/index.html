Bottom line: a broad option for email, forms, automation and integrations

Decision summary

Mailchimp

Mailchimp offers Free, Essentials, Standard and Premium. Paid pricing is determined by the feature plan and selected contact tier. The Free plan is limited to 250 contacts and 500 sends per month or 250 per day. Paid overages can apply when contact or send limits are exceeded, so both list size and campaign frequency should be modeled before purchase.

Officially documented advantages

  • Free supports basic campaign creation, forms and reporting
  • Paid plans list 3, 5 or unlimited seats depending on plan
  • The pricing page lists 300+ integrations
  • Contacts and broader account data can be exported

Constraints to verify

  • Sending is paused when the Free contact or send limit is exceeded
  • Unsubscribed and non-subscribed contacts may count toward paid contact limits
  • Paid plans can incur overage charges
  • Pricing varies by tier, promotion, currency and tax

When it may fit

Potential fit

Teams that want contacts, forms, campaigns, automation and integrations in one environment. Check the required seats and roles before choosing a plan.

When to compare alternatives

Organizations that send infrequently or want to avoid contact-based monthly pricing. Compare pay-as-you-go and send-volume pricing using the same projected list and send volume.

Frequently asked questions

Is Mailchimp free?

Yes. The official pricing page lists a Free plan with 250 contacts, 500 sends per month or 250 per day, one seat and one audience.

Is pricing based only on subscribers?

No. Paid pricing combines the plan and contact tier, with a monthly send limit. Subscribed, non-subscribed and unsubscribed contacts can count toward the contact limit.

Can data be exported?

Contacts can be exported to CSV. Account exports can include audiences, templates, marketing emails and SMS data.
