MailerLite vs Brevo: Subscribers, Send Volume, Automation, and Pricing

MailerLite prices around audience size and creator features; Brevo places more emphasis on message volume and multichannel communication.

Published / verified: July 19, 2026By Luqevora Editorial Team
Key decision

Decision summary

Choose MailerLite for a streamlined newsletter, landing-page, website, and creator workflow. Choose Brevo when a larger contact database, controlled send volume, transactional email, CRM, or SMS matters.

  • MailerLite: newsletters, sites, landing pages, automation, and paid newsletter tools
  • Brevo: campaigns, automation, transactional email, CRM, SMS, and send-volume plans
  • Both: authentication, segmentation, reporting, integrations, and plan limits

Pricing and plan limits

Forecast contacts and sends for 12 months, then add users, domains, transactional messages, dedicated IP, SMS, and migration work.

For MailerLite and Brevo, identify the lowest plan containing every required feature and compare the total for the full billing period.

  • Record introductory and renewal pricing separately
  • Compare monthly, annual, and longer-term totals
  • Check limits for seats, storage, sites, sends, or usage
  • Review refund, cancellation, and data-retention terms

Strengths and trade-offs

MailerLite is simpler but may need another service for complex transactional work; Brevo is broader but requires send-volume planning.

  • Strength: Different pricing models fit different list behavior
  • Strength: Both can be tested with a real small campaign
  • Verify: Free allowances and plan names can change
  • Verify: Sender authentication, consent, and export must be tested

Who it suits

Use the ratio of stored contacts to monthly sends as the first filter.

  • MailerLite: creators and newsletter-first small businesses
  • Brevo: larger lists and transactional messaging
  • Teams able to forecast list growth and sends

Implementation checklist

Pilot MailerLite and Brevo in a small scope and document cost, workflow, migration, and administration before rollout.

  • Define the objective and completion criteria in one sentence
  • Reproduce a representative task in a free plan or trial
  • Test administrator, contributor, and viewer permissions
  • Perform a real backup or export test
  • Record the renewal date and internal owner
