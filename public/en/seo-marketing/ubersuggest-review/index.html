Ubersuggest Review: Free Features, Pricing, Rank Tracking, Site Audits and Lifetime Plans

Bottom line: a candidate for managing one to fifteen sites with clearly stated monthly and Lifetime options

Decision summary

Ubersuggest

Ubersuggest combines keyword research, rank tracking, site audits, competitor and backlink research and AI-search visibility. The official pricing page lists Personal at $29 per month, Business at $49 and Enterprise at $99, with a one-time seven-day trial for monthly subscriptions and separate Lifetime purchase options.

Advantages confirmed from official sources

  • Limited free features are available for initial keyword research
  • Personal, Business and Enterprise list one, seven and fifteen projects
  • Rank tracking, site audits, competitors, backlinks and AI-search visibility are in one product
  • Monthly, annual and Lifetime purchase options are offered

Constraints to confirm before purchase

  • Free, trial and paid limits differ by searches, projects and keywords
  • The official FAQ states Lifetime purchases are final and non-refundable
  • The official FAQ states API access is not currently planned
  • Keyword volume and traffic figures are estimates from multiple data providers, not first-party measurements

When it may fit

Suitable when

Your site and keyword counts are known and you want keyword, ranking, audit, competitor and backlink functions with a lower monthly entry point.

Consider alternatives when

You require API integration, very large data volumes, daily crawling, complex agency reporting or extensive historical data.

Frequently asked questions

Can Ubersuggest be used free?

Limited free features are offered. Search and report limits may vary by account status, so confirm them in the live account.

Is there a free trial?

The official pricing FAQ states that monthly subscriptions receive a one-time seven-day free trial.

Are Lifetime purchases refundable?

The official FAQ states that Lifetime purchases are final and non-refundable, and recommends using the trial first.

Is API access available?

The official FAQ states that there are currently no plans to provide API access.
