How to compare price and plan limits

Shopify should be costed beyond the base plan, including payment and third-party transaction terms, apps, themes, POS, and operational add-ons.

WordPress.com starts free. Paid tiers add custom domains, ad removal, themes, plugins, backups, and commerce, with meaningful differences between monthly and multi-year billing.

Compare Shopify and WordPress.com by the annual total of the lowest plan that meets every required limit, not the headline price alone.

  • Introductory and renewal totals, including transaction fees
  • Limits for sites, products, storage, and traffic
  • Extra costs for domains, themes, and apps
  • Export coverage for content, products, and customer data

Run the same sample project in both products and record task time, friction, and extra cost.

  • Record introductory, renewal, and add-on totals

Frequently asked questions

Which is easier for beginners, Shopify or WordPress.com?

Best fit for Shopify: Businesses for which commerce is central and operational scale matters. Best fit for WordPress.com: Users who want WordPress extensibility with less server administration. Compare both under the same free or trial conditions.

Can I compare them using free plans?

A Shopify or WordPress.com free plan or trial can reveal the core workflow, but also check paid-plan limits, administration, export, and support on their official comparison pages.

What should I verify before migrating?

For Shopify and WordPress.com, verify export formats, history and attachment coverage, permission mapping, integration reauthorization, and the cancellation date for the old service.
