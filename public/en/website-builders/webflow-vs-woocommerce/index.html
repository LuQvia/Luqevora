How to compare price and plan limits

Webflow separates hosting for each published site from workspace collaboration. Its May 2026 plan changes and AI credits make it important to distinguish new pricing from legacy subscriptions.

Instead of one subscription, annualize hosting, backups, security, payments, paid extensions, production, and maintenance time. Include renewals and compatibility testing.

Compare Webflow and WooCommerce by the annual total of the lowest plan that meets every required limit, not the headline price alone.

  • Introductory and renewal totals, including transaction fees
  • Limits for sites, products, storage, and traffic
  • Extra costs for domains, themes, and apps
  • Export coverage for content, products, and customer data

Features and day-to-day workflow

Designers build layout and styles visually, then hand off reusable components and CMS collections for ongoing publishing.

Teams install WooCommerce on WordPress, configure products, payments, shipping, tax, and extensions, and own update testing, backups, and incident response.

  • Webflow: Fine-grained design control
  • Webflow: CMS and reusable components
  • Webflow: Integrated managed hosting
  • WooCommerce: Open-source control over the store and data
  • WooCommerce: Choice of hosting, payments, and features
  • WooCommerce: Integration with WordPress content

Which one should you choose?

Run one representative workflow centered on visual site and CMS production versus a WordPress store with selectable hosting and extensions in both products, then record completion time, revisions, and administrator setup.

  • Choose Webflow: Agencies and design teams that value fine visual control and a structured CMS
  • Choose WooCommerce: Stores that value WordPress flexibility and data control and can own technical operations internally or through a partner
  • Also test Shopify for this requirement: Businesses for which commerce is central and operational scale matters

Pre-purchase trial checklist

Run the same sample project in both products and record task time, friction, and extra cost.

  • Build the same page structure or sample catalog in both products
  • Check mobile rendering and the publishing workflow
  • Test the required domain, form, and payment setup
  • Perform a backup, duplicate, and export test
  • Record introductory, renewal, and add-on totals

Frequently asked questions

Which is easier for beginners, Webflow or WooCommerce?

Best fit for Webflow: Agencies and design teams that value fine visual control and a structured CMS. Best fit for WooCommerce: Stores that value WordPress flexibility and data control and can own technical operations internally or through a partner. Compare both under the same free or trial conditions.

Can I compare them using free plans?

A Webflow or WooCommerce free plan or trial can reveal the core workflow, but also check paid-plan limits, administration, export, and support on their official comparison pages.

What should I verify before migrating?

For Webflow and WooCommerce, verify export formats, history and attachment coverage, permission mapping, integration reauthorization, and the cancellation date for the old service.
