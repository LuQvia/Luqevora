Wix Review: Free Site, Custom Domains, Payments and Portability

This review organizes Wix’s free-site terms, custom-domain requirements, HTTPS/SSL, payments, site history and export boundaries using official documentation only. It does not assign an independent score for design quality or ease of use.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 10 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial Team

Method:We reviewed official pricing, help, security and migration documentation on July 16, 2026. Plan names, prices, availability and features can change, so reconfirm them at checkout.

Scope:This is an official-source review for purchase decisions. We did not perform a reproducible usability or performance test, so it does not rank ease of use, speed or design quality.

Conclusion: a candidate for small businesses that want site, forms, bookings and selling in one dashboard

Decision summary

Wix

Wix is a hosted platform that lets a business start on a free Wix URL and add a custom domain, bookings, forms and online selling through eligible paid plans. The complete Wix site cannot be exported to run on another host, so portability requirements should be checked before committing.

Advantages confirmed in official documentation

  • Build and publish on a free Wix URL
  • Manage domains, forms, bookings and selling within one service
  • HTTPS/SSL is built into Wix sites
  • Products, orders, contacts and CMS collections have feature-specific exports

Constraints to check before subscribing

  • The free site uses a Wix URL and Wix branding
  • A paid plan is required for a custom domain and branding removal
  • Payment availability and providers vary by region
  • A complete Wix site cannot be exported to another host

Review full-site portability separately from business-data exports

Website itself

Wix describes its platform as cloud SaaS and does not support exporting the complete Wix site to run on an external host. External site backups are not available; Site History and site duplication are the platform’s recovery options.

Business data

Products, orders, contacts and CMS collections can be exported through feature-specific tools. Some data types, including certain digital-product data, have limitations, so migration requirements should be checked field by field.

Operational note:Before launch, confirm how pages, posts, images, products, customers, orders, form responses and the domain can be recovered or transferred.

Who it may suit and who should compare alternatives

May suit

  • You want site, forms and bookings in one service
  • Non-developers need to update the site internally
  • A small store or service payments may be added
  • You want to prototype on a free URL before upgrading

Compare alternatives when

  • You require a complete HTML/CSS site export
  • Your team must control the server and source code
  • The project depends on extensive custom back-end development
  • Your payment, currency or country requirements are unsupported

Pre-purchase checklist

  1. 1. Decide whether the launch needs a Wix URL or a custom domain
  2. 2. List required forms, bookings, selling and membership features
  3. 3. Verify payment providers and fees for the business country
  4. 4. Identify products, orders and contacts that may need future export
  5. 5. Define collaborator permissions and offboarding procedures

Frequently asked questions

Can the free plan use a custom domain?

No. Wix states that connecting a custom domain and removing Wix branding require a paid plan.

Can a Wix site be moved to another server?

The complete Wix site cannot be exported to run on another host. Products, orders, contacts and CMS data may have separate export tools.

Can Wix accept online payments?

An eligible Premium or Studio site plan is required. Available providers and payment methods depend on the business location.
