WordPress.com Review: Free Plan, Plugins, Backups and Ecommerce

This review organizes WordPress.com’s free and paid plans, custom domains, plugins, SSL, backups, exports and WooCommerce using official documentation only. It distinguishes the hosted WordPress.com service from self-hosted WordPress.org.

Published and checked: July 16, 2026Estimated reading time: 10 minutesBy Luqevora Editorial Team

Method:We reviewed official pricing, help, security and migration documentation on July 16, 2026. Plan names, prices, availability and features can change, so reconfirm them at checkout.

Scope:This is an official-source review for purchase decisions. We did not perform a reproducible usability or performance test, so it does not rank ease of use, speed or design quality.

Conclusion: a candidate for publishing-focused businesses that want extensions and migration options

Decision summary

WordPress.com

WordPress.com is a hosted WordPress service. It has a permanent free plan, and the current official pricing page states that paid plans include a first-year domain, plugin installation, ad-free visitor experience and CDN hosting. Content and media can be exported, while automatic full-site backups are centered on Business and Commerce plans.

Advantages confirmed in official documentation

  • Start pages and posts on a free plan with no expiration
  • Current paid plans support plugins and custom themes
  • Export posts, pages and comments as XML and download the media library
  • Extend the site into a WooCommerce store

Constraints to check before subscribing

  • WordPress.com and self-hosted WordPress.org have different contracts and responsibilities
  • More plugins and themes create compatibility, update and cost management
  • Automatic backups and one-click restore are primarily Business and Commerce features
  • The standard XML export is not a full copy of themes, plugins and the database

Standard exports and complete backups serve different purposes

Content export

The standard export creates XML for posts, pages, comments and other supported content. The media library can be downloaded separately. This is primarily a content-migration workflow.

Backup and restore

Automatic backups and one-click restores are available on Business and Commerce. A standard XML file is not a complete restore package containing the theme, plugins and database.

Operational note:Before launch, confirm how pages, posts, images, products, customers, orders, form responses and the domain can be recovered or transferred.

Who it may suit and who should compare alternatives

May suit

  • You will publish articles or knowledge content continuously
  • Plugins and custom themes may be needed later
  • Content and media portability matters
  • A WooCommerce store is planned

Compare alternatives when

  • You do not want to manage plugin or theme decisions
  • You only want a tightly fixed editor with minimal configuration
  • Automatic full backups are required below Business
  • You have not distinguished WordPress.com from WordPress.org

Pre-purchase checklist

  1. 1. Choose explicitly between WordPress.com and self-hosted WordPress.org
  2. 2. Verify that required plugins and themes are supported by the current plan
  3. 3. If automatic backups are required, evaluate Business or Commerce
  4. 4. Confirm XML, media and form-response export workflows
  5. 5. For WooCommerce, estimate payments, shipping, tax and extension costs

Frequently asked questions

Does the WordPress.com free plan expire?

The official pricing page describes a free plan with no expiration.

Can paid plans install plugins?

The current official pricing and plan documentation states that Personal, Premium, Business and Commerce paid plans can use plugins. Reconfirm the feature at checkout.

Can a WordPress.com site be exported?

Posts, pages and comments can be exported as XML, and the media library can be downloaded separately. Moving themes and plugins requires an eligible plan and a separate full-site migration workflow.
