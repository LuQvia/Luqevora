How to compare price and plan limits

WordPress.com starts free. Paid tiers add custom domains, ad removal, themes, plugins, backups, and commerce, with meaningful differences between monthly and multi-year billing.

Wix can be tested with a free site, while a custom domain, removal of Wix branding, payments, storage, and collaboration depend on paid tiers and region.

Compare WordPress.com and Wix by the annual total of the lowest plan that meets every required limit, not the headline price alone.

  • Introductory and renewal totals, including transaction fees
  • Limits for sites, products, storage, and traffic
  • Extra costs for domains, themes, and apps
  • Export coverage for content, products, and customer data

Features and day-to-day workflow

Users manage WordPress posts, pages, themes, and plugins while the platform handles underlying hosting and maintenance.

Users can begin with a template or AI-generated site, edit pages visually, and add booking, forms, store, and marketing features.

  • WordPress.com: Integrated hosting and maintenance
  • WordPress.com: WordPress publishing model
  • WordPress.com: Plugin extensibility on paid tiers
  • Wix: Flexible visual placement
  • Wix: Broad business features
  • Wix: All-in-one site operations

Which one should you choose?

Test article publishing, a static-page edit, a new form, and a backup or export workflow in both products.

  • Choose WordPress.com for blogs, media, long-term content accumulation, and WordPress extensions
  • Choose Wix for small-business sites, free-form visual editing, and fast deployment of bookings or stores
  • Evaluate self-hosted WordPress.org separately if your team can manage hosting and maintenance

Pre-purchase trial checklist

Run the same sample project in both products and record task time, friction, and extra cost.

  • Build the same page structure or sample catalog in both products
  • Check mobile rendering and the publishing workflow
  • Test the required domain, form, and payment setup
  • Perform a backup, duplicate, and export test
  • Record introductory, renewal, and add-on totals

Frequently asked questions

Which is easier for beginners, WordPress.com or Wix?

Wix is easier for a beginner who wants a visual launch quickly. WordPress.com is a strong starting point for someone building a long-lived blog and learning WordPress.

Can I compare them using free plans?

A WordPress.com or Wix free plan or trial can reveal the core workflow, but also check paid-plan limits, administration, export, and support on their official comparison pages.

What should I verify before migrating?

For WordPress.com and Wix, verify export formats, history and attachment coverage, permission mapping, integration reauthorization, and the cancellation date for the old service.
